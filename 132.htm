Today has been one of the worst days of my life. One of the very worst days of my life. Lots of people who care about me, or who supposedly care about me have caused me lots of grief and pain. Maybe even I hurt some of them, I can't really be sure, but it seems to me that it was the other way around. I would so gladly tell you all about it, but I fear that the literal confession might seem extremely banal and embarrassing.

A writer once said that, in a world full of injustice and sadness, only desire for death can be perceived as a just solution. Seen how I'm extremely low on my self-esteem at the moment and even on the verge of a nervous break-down, I'm gonna agree with that statement. That doesn't necessarily mean that I want to die (at all), although I actually thought about a minute ago about the credo "Your Funeral ... My Trial" being one true reflection of my life, more accurate than any other I could possible think of (which implies that I would be ready to deprive someone of life and then suffer for it, well at least "in theory"). It sucks however you put it. The funny thing is that, looking from the outside, my life might seem perfect to some; a friend, in fact, complained recently about being in quite a mess, juxtaposing his own "hopeless" position with my, as he had put it, carefree and joyful life. And here I am, sitting in front of my computer now, laughing at those words, listening to Nick Cave, one author who constantly invokes the salvation of death, thinking about what could have been done differently, and dying inside. Is that the impression I give? A perfect life? As if it ever even could be... Moreover, to a friend. I guess I am one hell of an actor.

July the 14 is the day I wish had never happened. I wish I could erase it from my memory forever, go back and make different decisions. The first consequences are still too palpable and painful to make me perceive their true aftermath in the long run. And I try... you know, I really try to remember that people suffer all around me, in different ways and for various reason, yet it doesn't seem to really ease my own pain. That's how today's has been to me. I'm not even sure that I'll learn anything valuable form today's failures. At least I can do is prey that such a day shall never repeat ever again...

ozzystylez Feb 05 2009
+5 Vote downBookmarkVote up
The British public's reaction to heavy snowfall would be seen by many of you northern hemisphere dwellers from Canada, Russia, Switzerland and the Arctic circle as laughable and irrationally paranoid. Since the snow fell here on Sunday night the traffic systems have been plunged into chaos as an otherwise unheard of grit crisis threatens to turn the roads into an ice rink and leave the working population, as well as the participants in the educational systems, housebound and unable to function. Because no-one would ever dream of walking anywhere anymore.

The onset of the current "snow crisis" does make a refreshing change from the "deepening economic crisis" in the headlines. The more skilful of the news corporations have managed to marry the two, spotting the opportunity for a combined theme of misery and depression running between the two. Reports of smaller companies struggling because parents are having to take days off work because their children can't go to school make for dull tea-time viewing. Regardless, the local news stations can't get enough of this "bad" weather talk, and are turning the more paranoid among us into jittery cloud watchers.

Not all of us see this rare weather as bad. Waking up in the morning to a pure white world untouched by any sign of life is both comforting and beautiful. Those of us who still have enough lust for life to walk to our daily destinations are treated to a very quiet and still world. It is one which feels soft, like the ringing in your ears the night after a loud concert, and is something of a rare treat. Walking fresh, crunchy tracks across an untouched white field isn't something that you get to do every day, not even every year. Not enough people are embracing the rare beauty of rolling white countryside.

I understand the down sides to the snow. Your ability to leave the town you live in is hindered; loved ones can seem farther away and getting to see them or them coming to see you is risky and so the snow can feel a little isolating. A young girl died in a sledging accident the other day (although a young boy also died in a washing machine accident in the same week. Perhaps the misadventures of kids should be blamed as opposed to the cruel cold front blowing in on Siberian winds). Car crashes are on the rise. As I walked to work this morning the only cars that were on the road were wheel spinning down the camber and into the gutter where they will be trapped until the thaw. Authorities are telling us to only make essential journeys and to take plenty of food with us in the car in case we get stranded. There is also black snow which is the ugly, evil twin of white snow, pure snow. Black snow lost its virginal blush and picked up all the dirt from the street. It can often be found rolled up into a giant snowball which stands as an immovable monument to filth.

It is very tempting to disturb the untouched snow wherever it may gather; on walls, on people’s drives, on the branches of trees. But once we have scratched the itch that compels us to disrupt the claustrophobic beauty of a white morning, we are a little less in awe of it; it is tainted, and we did it. It is still beautiful but our own desires destroyed the peace and it becomes another mark upon the earth that humans, unable to bear the itching of our flesh, have left. Until it melts away or is filled in again with second chance snow.

All of these musings on snow feed into Your Funeral...My Trial with surprising synchronicity. It is not an album that would be a natural choice for summer listening. It is cold, dark and sinister and the feel good factor is, in true Nick Cave style, inconspicuous in its absence. Yet on a cold and white day when the parks are deserted and the roads are an uncertain and sudden danger, where a soft, disquieting beauty cloaks potential horror and threatens to defeat us all and keep us bed bound like paranoid hermits, it seems like the perfect album to keep you warm. It was written for such times, such madness, such fear and such beauty. It is uncertain and alien, but warm and welcoming. It is a wintery album for wintery souls.

Sí, con inteligencia, olvidamos la horrorosa idea de hacer un disco con canciones de otros artistas (¡Cave no es Rod Stewart, maldición!), este es el tercer disco «real» de nuestro héroe maldito; uno en el cual consolida su manera de componer, expande su abanico estilístico e incorpora a sus singulares modos de expresión desarrollados con éxito en «From Her to Eternity» y «The Firstborn Is Dead» un exquisito condimento melódico, suaves rasgueos de guitarra y cálidos pianos-órganos Hammond. Coloca su voz en un registro más natural y relega el gruñido desafiante del pasado a un papel secundario y aleatorio, aunque útil y excitante cuando es necesario. Cave aborda sus composiciones con un humor agrio y juguetón que revela su aplomo: la delicada balada homónima, la calma reflexiva de «Stranger Than Kindness», la gótica e inquietante «She Fell Away», el groove hipnótico de «Hard On for Love» y la atmósfera picaresca-teatral (de Cabaret) de «The Carny», dan cuenta de esto. Con «Your Funeral ... My Trial» Nick Cave consagra su estatus de artista de culto que prescinde de las modas y las tendencias en boga, incluso del sentir alternativo, pero, sobre todo, nutre su estilo, desde una óptica formal, de nuevos componentes estéticos-sonoros que se hallan dispersos y reforzados en su obra posterior, unánimemente aclamada: la de los años 90.

Nick Cave Retrospective: Your Funeral ...My Trial
with nick's first three albums, even though i really liked the firstborn is dead, it felt like they were part of a process for developing a signature style while still pairing it with good songwriting. your funeral ...my trial finally sees the group's sound finally establishing itself (as i know it right now, reminder that this is my first time going through this discog).

for one, the gothic atmosphere doesn't get in the way of the songwriting on this album like it did on from her to eternity, which makes songs like the title track and "stranger than kindess" stick more in my mind than past nick cave songs. another thing to note is the emphasis on pianos on the album. while other nick cave albums had some piano on them, especially on the covers album kicking against the pricks, there's much more focus on them and they really feed into the songwriting strengths i mentioned earlier. biggest example of both of these, and my favorite track on the album, is "the carny". it really does feel so unique compared to every track on the album, mainly consisting of this distorted piano melody while nick goes into grisly detail about circus workers finding one of their own workers dead alongside a sickly horse belonging to said worker.

the other albums before this one are good but this definitely feels like the first important album in the nick cave discography.

FAV TRACKS: Sad Waters, The Carny, Your Funeral My Trial, Stranger Than Kindness, Jack's Shadow, Long Time Man
LEAST FAV TRACK: Hard On for Love
