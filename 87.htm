Oh my god
Alright this wasn't planned, but I was at my brother's place, and I was high as shit and I listens the first seven songs with my brother, and my sister in law, cause she grew up with Blink-182 and I was mentioning how I started listening to them. She than said this album was great, so I put it on, and we'll see was right, I didn't finish all of it tell they dropped me off, and I listened to the rest while reading Scott Pilgrim. Anyway, yeah this is great, it takes, coming off of 'Enema of State' like this shit is fucking great, my god.


It's funny cause I don't like 'Anthem' on Enema of State, but 'Anthem Part Two' is a much better song, 'Online Songs' is fucking great, I love the bass on this especially, it's a lot more up front, 'First Date' is an instant classic, just fucking spectacular honestly, 'Story of a Lonely Guy's quickly became a favorite from this album,the background vocals are great, I also love how it's not super punk, it's a little more punk rock than anything which I love, but it's got a badass guitar riff when it comes to the verse, which I fucking love.

The next track I only knew by name , but 'The Rock Show' does indeed rock, also the bass is fucking perfect, and the chorus is brilliant. 'Roller Coaster as this trippy moment from 1:37-2:02 I had no idea what it was but it was weird, and it's cool, cause I like the lyrics. Then 'Reckless Abandon' comes in, and I can't stop moving my feet, and air drumming, just a fun fast song, the opening of ' Give me on Good Reason' is fantastic, I also love Travis's playing in it. I fucking love 'Shut Up' so goddamn much it is my favorite track of this alum, the lyrics are fantastic, and the chorus cooks with diesel fuel.


Then the last song, I mean it's different in all the best ways, it's a mix of everything,and I love it, just a pure banger.

Yes this is the best time we've ever had

Other tracks like 'Stay Together For the Kids' is a little too emo for me, and same for 'Happy Holidays you bastard, luckily they don't bring down the album at all, cause the rest cooks... I was wrong about 'Everytime I Look At You' that shit is awesome.

More of the same bullshit from Enema of the State. This album might be arguably worse. Even Tom's guitar work took a nosedive on here. The riffs sound overproduced, dull and just horrible. I think the only song where the band show any potential is Online Songs, especially in the verses where there's an admittedly interesting riff, but that's the only thing I can compliment on this album. Mark and Tom's vocals sound awful and even autotuned as usual. Tom's nasally voice combined with Mark's one-octave deep voice just doesn't blend. The lyrics are the same shit from the previous album about relationships with some chick who will dump you in a year because you cheated on her with her mom. None of the lyrics are well-written. In fact, a lot of the lyrics are excessively cheesy. Really awful album.

aged like fine wine
All my teenage years were full of Blink-182 tracks, as I’m sure everyone else’s was too. I always found challenging going back to what I used to listen to when I was 16/17 ‘cause something that you would think it’s epic at that age loses its magic when you’re a grown up, and that applies especially to pop punk bands such as Blink themselves. The genre can sometimes pass as monotonous and shallow too. But when I listened to this album once again last summer I was surprisingly pleased to find it exactly as genuine, authentic and real as I remember it to be. There are no skips, the tracklist swiftly passes through fast paced songs and mellow ones, the sound is seamlessly coherent without feeling like is just one song repeated slightly different every time.

Getting turned on to this album in 6th grade was a game changer for me. At that age, an album like this with lots of cursing and crass lyrics about sex hit the perfect spot with just-about-to-hit-puberty me. For that reason alone, this will always be a highly nostalgic album for me. I've been working on making playlists of the most memorable songs of each decade, which brought me back to Take off Your Pants and Jacket. Listening to it this morning as a 31 year old, it's actually not making me cringe too much. This record is loaded with catchy, fun songs. No it doesn't have the most insightful lyrics. However, it is the peak of Blink 182's mix of stupid fun and light adolescent angst.
