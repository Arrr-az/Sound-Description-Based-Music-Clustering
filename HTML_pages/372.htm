SandyMc Jul 25 2006
+11 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Power, Corruption And Lies arrived at a pivotal time in my life, when it's exquisitely packaged themes of loss, sadness and isolation were able to make a direct hit to my heart. Four tracks in particular had that delicate intelligence, uniqueness of style and emotional rush which made them as good as anything I'd ever heard. "Age Of Consent" is highly charged power pop, or maybe electric folk; "The Village", the album's one truly joyful track, is pure pop, but so English, arty and progressive. "Your Silent Face" turned the "disco sound" into a sublime experience of introverted, understated beauty that was truly art rock for the eighties; and "Leave Me Alone" closed the album on an epiphany of pure multi-layered guitar-pop perfection.

New Order had an unparalleled knack for crafting simple melodies that pricked the heart like rose thorns, and lodged in the brain permanently. It was a gift that was to make them my favourite band of the eighties. The band was grieving the loss of their close friend Ian, a fact that is quietly but explicitly stated in this recording. Consequently there is a real sense of death and renewal about this album. It was during the time of this album that I literally, truly found God. As a postscript, I've always believed that "Leave Me Alone" was "borrowed" for The Dream Academy's beautiful hit, "Life In A Northern Town." One of my favourite albums.

Hawkeyes89 Oct 15 2011
+2 Vote downBookmarkVote up
New Order's sophomore effort, Power, Corruption, & Lies stands as the best album by the band and one of the defining albums of both the synth pop and new wave genres. For reviewing purposes, I'm including the single "Blue Monday" which while not released with the original album has appeared on subsequent versions. The opening track is the principle reason for why I've decided to rate and review New Order's discography. Before last year I was only familiar with "Blue Monday" and "Bizarre Love Triangle" but "Age of Consent" stands as my all time favorite track by the band. Simple, catchy (a little bit repetitive) and washed in poppy synthesizers makes this an all time great for New Wave. Best of all, it signals that this is not going to be another half hearted attempt to rejuvenate the sound Sumner and Co. wore out with their previous band Joy Division.

Aside from a more upbeat sound Sumner's vocals come into their own on this album. He doesn't have what many would consider a classically good singing voice and it (intentionally?) cracks at times. Personally I'm drawn to unique singers who can carry a tune and Sumner is such a frontman. What follows is a tasteful application of synthesizers and a distinctly 80's guitar sound mixed with the early makings of house music. "The Village" another track in the mold of "Age of Consent" and "5 8 6" is a nice suite of songs that I wouldn't expect from a band like New Order. It's a tactic I rarely applaud but each segment is distinct while working well with the other components of the track to make the suite approach worthwhile.

"Blue Monday" is of course the centerpiece of the album is is the track New Order are probably best known for. One of the greatest synth pop tracks I own it's haunting groove and melancholy lyrics without losing pace is an impressive acomplishment. The second half is a step down from the first but still contains some highlights. "Your Silent Face" incorporates a gorgeous sythesizer line and "Leave Me Alone" is another high point despite being a bit "by the numbers". Overall this is the logical starting point for any New Order collection and tops Brotherhood as the best album by the band.

Favorites: "Age of Consent" "The Village" "Blue Monday"

Not My Favorite But Has a Personality
As the title says, this is not my favorite record in this genre. That being said, it definitely has it's own distinct flavor that helps it distinguish itself from others. The synth work is really beautiful and the bass lines are killer. What sets it apart from other albums in this genre, such as Drums and Wires, is the lack of memorable vocal and guitar work. They're both perfectly fine throughout, don't get me wrong, but they don't have the flair or have as much fun experimenting as I'd of liked them too.

With all that being said, this is still a good record and I can see why it would work a lot more for others. "Age of Consent" is an all time new wave track also.

ok-good
I appreciate the eclectic nature of this album, but it's aged so poorly to my ears. The early electronic stuff just sounds like 'old man beats' at times.

The moments where they go more Indie are where it really hits for me. But I'm not really surprised.

Guitar tones are real nice throughout and the interplay between them and some of the more textural synth sounds, as the rest of the composition continues to build around them, keeps things moving and keeps this album from getting too tiresome.

They push these bittersweet yet groovy melodies and chord progressions, infectious bright lead synths and moving bass-lines, but lack a truly ear catching hook.

6/10
