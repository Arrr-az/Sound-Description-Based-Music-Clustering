Pet Sounds is a record that reads like a love struck teenagers diary and is played with the temperament of a child looking forward to growing up, whilst at the same time fearing the changes that lie ahead of him. It is a record that knows nothing of sex, a virgins dreams if you like, not in a randy, longing way like one desperate to remove the chastity belt and be ravished until mollified, but in a way that has not been let in on the secrets of the birds and the bees and does not look forward to sexual gratification because he doesnt know it exists. There is nothing in the lyrics to Wouldnt It Be Nice that suggests that the singer wants to be older for any other reason than not having to leave his betrothed to go home to separate houses each evening. There is no mention of running away from their parents watchful gaze in order to snatch a few breathless moments of soft core action, the singer just wants to be married and be together after goodnights have been said.

God Only Knows is perhaps the purest love song on this or any other record. It is a world away from the blue innuendo of modern chart pop love, there is no emphasis on the be in We should be together or I want to be with you girl. Love, to the by then befuddled mind of Brian Wilson, comes without the lewd complications which sex can bring. In fact life itself, in Wilsons ideal world, comes without the problems and complications of growing old. Thats Not Me sees the protagonist venturing out into the city but finds himself longing for the suburbs. I Just Wasnt Made for These Times sees him turning against the hedonism of the sixties and wishing himself back to the simpler fifties where, as we can see by the movies and anti-communist propaganda of that decade, things were simpler, more idealistic and happier.

So Pet Sounds can be seen as an album that is a dream dreamt by a simple young man who is afraid of growing up into an era he either doesnt understand or doesnt think hell enjoy. It has a magical quality to it, the instruments dont sound like instruments, not in the way that the Beatles wanted to achieve on Revolver, these instruments just dont sound familiar, they make up the noise of a sunny afternoon daydream, an untouchable place where the unknown doesnt figure in the equation and the familiar, warm and fluffy comfort of simplistic adolescence rules the airwaves. It is perhaps the beginning of Wilsons mental collapse but in that instability lies the brilliance and beauty of his vision.

Many things have been said about Pet Sounds, but I think one detail gets overlooked time and time again about the whole experience. Chiefly that Brian Wilson described it as his attempt at teenage hymns, or however it is that the quote went. Doesn't matter, he definitely used the word "hymn" and I think that's really the key here. My first listen to Pet Sounds was an immediate smash success, probably brought upon by the all out assault of great melody after my many years of hip-hop rhythm centered listenings. Coming back to it here and later I had to do more work than I had expected to reaffirm my initial awe, but that too makes sense. Melody wasn't going to bowl me over now that I had been surrounded my melody for four years straight. I think maybe I'd gotten it in my head as well to expect bouncy pop, a mistaken memory or perhaps actually a result of me going through all their previous records this time. But Pet Sounds isn't simply more Beach Boys pop but better, it's a whole other thing. Pop even seems to be beside the point half the time, Brian isn't straining to make the best possible pop here. The man is after something a bit different. The key attribute going on here is the Wall of Sound production style, straight outta Phil Spector land. Being of the early 60's the Beach Boys had always had a bit of that genetic in their sound but here it gets clearly taken up a deliberate notch. I have nothing but good feelings toward the Wall sound, but I can rarely narrow down precisely what it is I like. Part of it might be it's urban denseness, the earliest incarnation of urban in rock/pop music perhaps (depending how you feel on jazz I guess). But that urban almost proto-noise rock thing is definitely not a factor in the Beach Boys at any stage, so it's use here isn't working because of that. No it's possibly in large parts the BIGNESS of the sound, but the way it's used here? Well back to the hymn idea we go. I'm no religious man but the power of a big church hymn to shake the walls with it's organs and bombast is just basic music appreciation on some level. Used to great effect up to the present millennium in great songs like Arcade Fire's Intervention. It pulls on emotional heartstrings easily and powerfully, it evokes images in our heads of things bigger than ourselves. Doesn't need to be about any gods at all, just like here where it's in some ways most of the same down to earth boy/girl trouble cuteness. Well let's not be too light on that, as was happening over the course of the last record? Well here Brian has leaned the lyrics and song content all the way into something more emotionally substantial. Not always romance, or perhaps always romance but just not always romance in the love sense. Because oh yeah is this all the product of a hopeless romantic...perhaps a little too hopeless for his own good long term. So think about that. The songs are now sculpted to near perfection in their earnest direct subject, and now the music has been crafted as well to fit that mold and blow them up to their expected proportions. The music now matches the mood. Result? A jump so tremendous that even having chronologically gone through their material it seems super sudden. Today was great, but it doesn't provide the natural point for this. This speaks for itself. And no a simple love for pop in the more quick and light sense won't really win you points here. Pet Sounds is stately and moves at precisely the slower more stargazey pace it wants to move on. The melodies are gorgeous as hell yes, but it intends on making you sit and get mesmerized by the atmosphere. Even within the usually two to three minute time frames.

10

Back when my adolescent music snobbery was developing and I was schooling my junior high classmates on the importance of the fab four on modern music, I never thought I would put a fun-in-the-sun Beach Boys album above any of my cherished Beatles music, much less all of them. I mean where would The Beatles be without it.

An innocent spirituality that has outlasted most of its contemporaries with relative ease. The stomach needs food, the heart needs blood, the soul needs Pet Sounds.
