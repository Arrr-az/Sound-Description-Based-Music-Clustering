Having read through previous reviews, I feel once again compelled to note that this record came out in 1983. At that time this was shockingly NEW. At the time it simply knocked one on one's ass.

After almost three decades of imitators of the early industrial/experimental bands, the excitement this innovation in sound produced at the time cannot be recaptured, and those who were not of an age to appreciate it at the time may never understand the visceral force and excitement they produced. Sure later versions of the form were better produced, more polished (?!), but the innovators deserve the credit. Something similar can also be said about early techno, and a few earlier forms that I was too young to experience (early rock music...).

So few real innovators these days.

SWANS: The Perilous Journey, Chapter I: FILTH
DAY ONE, "Stay Here":
"Last night I retired to my comfy bed. Today, I awake in a dark room with a dirt floor. I could be wrong, but the room feels very big, and mostly empty. But in the corner, there was a man eating a pneumatic drill, and in the opposite corner, a man fucking a bag of mulch. The sound of a million teeth gritting at once was somehow the most erotic sensation, and the gargling of oil tickled my perverted eardrums. I wasn't tied down, shackled, of anything of that sort. I was just sitting there on the dirt floor, in an angry trance. I felt the odd urge to prove myself to the puny Gods. Fascinating."

DAY TWO, "Big Strong Boss":
"A man with grotesque teeth and a dick like a python tied to a bigger python walked ominously out of his own hatefog, and put his hand under my chin and pushed my head up, my eyes aligned with what should be his eyes. I wanted to recoil, but I could tell that he could and would spit razorblades at me. So I simply sat there, smiled nervously and waited. He did indeed proceed to spit at me. But what he spit was not sharp or bladelike in any manner. It wasn't quite sludge, but close. He made odd sounds and proceeded to lick the left side of my face. He gathered the room into a clatter, or something. Exciting. Will update"

DAY THREE, "Blackout":
"Crack, crick. Crick, crack. His neck said these words in that order. This man was full of false dreams and immoral aura. And mange. I could smell that. He proceeded to rip off his white tanktop, to reveal an off-white tanktop, and donned a cowboy hat of undetermined origin. He then, with his boar-like moan, coughed up something resembling two-thirds of someone else's lung. Was this a toolshed? Hmm. The nasty sizzle of genitals was potent in my senses. This was an uprising, I said. Whoops. The man turned his throbbing neck, pointed his bony finger at me and said "Don't talk until you're spoken to!" My erection was hushed in a witch's instant. More to come"

DAY FOUR, "Power for Power"
"I saw the rise of a black curtain in the blacker mist. But not really. Crunch-a-munch-a-zunga-urghus. That was my current mood. The nothing exploded into a greater nothing. Am I being swallowed by something beyond words? Seems it. The leader (I'm going to call him Cowboy Hat from here on out) sweat so profusely, he was becoming the toolshed. The corners were almost in the center of the room at this point, the way those slaves kneaded their gray/grey dough of hate. Like clockwork. Metal arms couldn't tear away this chokehold. I gulped down my thoughts and made sure my 500-yard stare never broke focus of Cowboy Hat. He lead the mush into mash and made the industry of natives turn sour at the tip of a hat. A Cowboy Hat. So that's why they call him that. And then..it happened."

DAY FIVE, "Freak"
"Cowboy Hat swung his head clockwise ("always clockwise", he pretended to mutter) at such a viciously fast rate that he pulled the entire room apart. Rust turned to dust, and vice versa. "You're gonna murder somebody", he said, lashing his tongue in directions that no one has ever heard of. "Violence and abuse". What a chant. Sleazy and defiant. Like a trashcan full of desires. I rose from the dirt floor and nodded my head, making sure each drop of blood in my head flowed exactly right. Cowboy Hat could sense these things."

DAY SIX, "Right Wrong"
"If the little drummer boy went Parum pa pum pum, the big drummer boy just swung his dick at bystanders and moaned. This I was assured. Cowboy Hat stomped on the ground and whispered a silence so fierce it became a noise. He pushed roughly into the dirt, almost swirling it into a ground dough. The slaughter seemed eternal. A Rube Goldberg machine of extreme energy and concentrated flesh. At the end, a massive opening. Or a miniscule closing. These happenings are rarely so black and white. And as I peered into the abyss, I saw a lack of nothing. A wave of nothing, if you will. Cowboy Hat proceeds to tear away his off-white tanktop, revealing an entire, drab brown suit. He then put his grimy submissive lady-skin boots the back of my neck, and gave me a push into the hole. He and the rest of the clatter followed. I don't know if the clatter had names"

DAY SEVEN, "Thank You"
"Chug chug chug away went Cowboy Hat. Clumps of cannibal incantations arose in the air like mid-level emotional relapse. I think this is what people call an alternate plane. I began to move my lips, to talk and everything, when Cowboy Hat covered my mouth (not just one finger, entire palm, with fingers gripping lips, like some sort of kinky clamp). I turned back around. I saw melting memories and intensely vivid pieces of vague ideas. Forgive me for the lack of specificity. I was still very fatigued. Or something. The end (or new beginning?) was near. I closed my eyes, uncertain"

DAY EIGHT, "Weakling":
"We circled our mental drain with furious insecurity, regressing into various flavored nonsense. The exit of all that was and will be (or was be) is over. Now is what is, and I am terrifrightened to my very last bone and organ. Cowboy Hat shreds his torso with a gardening tool. Just cuz. We approach an empty deserted train car, and Cowboy Hat jumps in, rushing me and the clatter inside. The journey deepens, as the blackest of sparrows circles the theater in our minds."

DAY NINE, "Gang":
"In my past, I was a nothing/nobody rambling the shifty confines of my garbled mental cage. I needed a salvation. Cowboy Hat was that salvation. I needed not love and desire. I needed rational understanding. I needed picturesque detailed reality. I needed a wholistic perspective of genuine life. I needed filth."

Existence really is a pain in the ass no matter where you live. If you live in an advanced capitalist country, you have to put up with constant greed, stupid peoples trivialities, idiotic politicians, and a disgusting culture of consumerism and hypocrisy. If you live in a less developed country, you have to put up with constant suffering, lack of necessities, and a myriad of other horrid issues. Both have such awful problems, both can be constant hell. This is especially true when you witness all the other humans around you making things worse through their stupid habits, their stupid actions, their stupid emotionalism. Everyone is making a mess of the planet, and idiocy worms its way into all societies. It’s just downright disgusting. People who see this can choose to ignore it. They can choose to fight against it and try to improve the world. Or in Swans case, they can just get really goddamn nasty about it all, and release one of the most uncompromisingly dark, festering and filthy albums of all time.

Filth slithered out of darkness in 1983, a time when the most popular underground music was actually fairly dark, or at least melancholic and less amused than most other music out there. Post-punk was still in its heyday as evident by releases that year from The Chameleons, New Order, The Fall, and U2 among others. Metal artists like Mercyful Fate and Slayer released albums that year. And of course, industrial was rising with artists like Einsturzende Neabauten. Maybe it was something in the water, but 1983 was not the year to be cheery in music it seems. So who would carry the torch of hatred and emotional instability that year? Against the backdrop of the Cold War in an industrialized, capitalist society, Swans decided to carry that black lit torch. In doing so, they lit fire to nearly all other artists who claimed to be dark at the time. Filth was released as a pounding, pummeling, noisy, and discordant no-wave meets industrial album, and it was just fucking beautiful.

The torch carrier and front-man of this aural assault is the great Michael Gira, who screams and shouts songs of such raw emotion and intensity that at times it may almost seem unbearable unless in the proper mood. Gira managed to put his soul on a dirty plate, throw it at the listener and say here’s your damn dinner; can you handle the taste of it? Songs are violent and aggressive, often demanding the listener, demanding humanity. “Come back for more! Do what I say! Use hatred for freedom! Use sex for power! Flex your muscle! Satisfy the dog!” Gira sees all the disgusting elements of humanity and sounds like he’s commanding you keep up that behavior; so that humanity can destroy itself. The message is nihilistic, it’s misanthropic, it’s full of hatred for society and for oneself. Much of the time Gira yells and screams, it sounds not only like he is directing his anger sadistically toward you, but also toward himself. Songs like “Stay Here” where Gira frantically screams “come back for more!” remind me of some sort of desperate attempt to establish a relationship through violence, self-loathing, and depression. It’s all rather disturbing if one isn’t up for that kind of subject matter. But when you’re pissed, when you’re so angry you feel like carving holes into humanity’s skull, this is the album to listen to.

In some way, it’s hard to say whether this is truly Swans most brutal album. The next few were not as fast paced and “groovy” as this album; the basslines weren’t as noticeably post-punk inspired per se. The compilation album “Body to Body” is in my opinion, perhaps the best match for “Filth,” with such songs like “Mother, My Body Disgusts Me” being slow, haunting, and featuring less yelling on Gira’s part, but more depressive moaning. Of course for those familiar with Swans, the band eventually toned things down. They became far more influenced by neofolk and goth-rock and eventually became a precursor to post-rock music. Gira was influenced heavily by later Swans member Jarboe, and while that has meant the hatred of “Filth” has been lost, their new material is still quite dark, intelligent, and utterly beautiful, often in cinematic quality. Whatever one may think of the newer Swans material, this album remains incredibly unique and a landmark in aggressive music. The influence it had on artists like Godflesh and Napalm Death is huge. Hell, most metal musicians today sure as hell still could take a lesson or two from Swans. So for when you’re in the mood for some skull crushing hatred, listen to “Filth.” I guarantee you will come back for more.

An incredible barrage of clattering clanking percussion and buzzing scraping guitar, flavoured occasionally by squalling feedback and Michael Gira's general sense of disgust at consumer culture/wage slavery/the human race and so on and so forth......

BE HARD

FLEX YOUR MUSCLES

GET DRUNK

USE SEX FOR CONTROL

SATISFY THE DOG

Now thats one hell of a motivational to-do list. I can imagine Gira giving a power-point presentation with those phrases prominently displayed throughout.



Superficially as ugly and unrelenting as the rest of early Swans records, this stands out for me personally by being just that little bit more rhythmically dynamic, combined with a more pronounced and aggressive form of guitar abuse from Mr Norman Westberg.

My favourite part of the album is when the 70-odd second noise-hardcore "Freak" leads into the stupendous "Right Wrong", a song which contains everything that is brilliant about this record.

The albums and EPs to follow had their own impressive levels of intenisty and unpleasantness (and were mostly briliant also), but even by Swans standards _Filth_ sounds particularly violent and unhinged.
