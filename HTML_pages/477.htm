ZwielichtEnnui Jan 02 2013
+5 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Real Life by Magazine is one of those dark, quirky, alleyway albums that will entertain you all throughout your life.

The music extremely catchy with its innovative sound - the lead singer, Howard Devoto, sounds like some crazed pedophile. The lyrics are extremely witty yet accessible.

The album has a killer opener in Definitive Gaze. A sort of prelude to the album, with Devoto's perverted voice, it just lures you into the music. Listen to the dark keyboards mixed with the suggestively joyful chorus. Straight after Gaze comes My Tulpa, a much more happy song, but possessing it's only punk attitude.

Following My Tulpa is the classic highlight, Shot By Both Sides. Part of this album's quality lies in its twisted vocals, and this track is the perfect example. After this, the album relaxes a bit, though it doesn't really lose its quality. Motorcade seems to bring back the carnival sinister tones. Followed by The Great Beautician In The Sky, the album closes with another killer pair in The Light Pours Out Of Me and Parade.

This album is for all the brooding, night owls who can't be bothered to make something out of their lives, and yet want a bit of cynicism to get on with their lives.

antonbildern Sep 27 2009
+3 Vote downBookmarkVote up
It's shameful that Magazine still aren't considered one of the best acts of the post-punk period. Howard Devoto led one of the shortest career bands (Buzzcocks) and ten thousands years away from the punk hooliganism casted a handful of creative musicians to form Magazine. Always loved his aristocratic posture of intelligent leader, his alien-like figure standing silently in the shadowy line, sober but marked by tragedy. Listening Magazine it's like to be in the presence of an idiosyncratic and exhilarating blend of Peter Hammill's tragoedia, Brian Eno's lush quirkiness and post-punk energy.

And this remastered 2007 edition is the one I was expecting ever since, for bringing clearness to the group's dark baroque sound, holding the main attention to the outstanding work of Dave Formula, the keyboard player. He's the one who adds some shady effects to the album's darkly ice-age atmosphere (should we say atmosfear?). "Real Life" is like a steely cathedral surrounded by snow. And is also a terrifying study on loneliness. At those times, only Wire has reached the same detailed level of enthrallment. At those times, Magazine was no fuckin' jokin'...

"Definitive Gaze" and "My Tulpa" are two formidable openers to win an album. "My Tulpa" is the best keyboard-driven song on post-punk years. And then the rawer "Shot by Both Sides" recalls some of art-punk from Buzzcocks. It's definitely a 'thick as a (concrete) brick' new type of sound - probably they are going to be best remembered in a next future, much more than their contemporaries. To prove this, check the painful romanticism of "Burst" and "Motorcade", the gutsy-ghostly waltz of "The Great Beautician in the Sky" (sure that Peter Hammill is here not only in spirit...) and above all the stupendous gloomy bass/drum and knifelike guitar on "The Light Pours Out of Me" (how such a raged beauty!) or the melancholic existentialism of "Parade" (gosh, those shivering keyboards!), the album's closing track. A masterpiece.

Come dark music to bring us autumn days...

Look, I only bought this last month while visiting Indianapolis. But that hasn't stopped me from walking around with all this swagger and bravado. When I meet peons on the street, and they're all like "Astropolis, yo, you're a pimp, dogg!" and they want to come over to my crib and try on my clothes and be my best friend and shit... I come back at them with "Do you own and love the Magazine album, _Real Life_?" These sucker MCs stop, scratch their heads a minute, and come back with "Um, never heard of them." I smack these fools upside the head and walk on. I may have only had this album a short time, but that doesn't stop me from walking around, acting like I've been in the know since day one.

Buy this album, you instantly turn into what one sage described as an "old soul". Listen to "The Light Pours Out Of Me" and tell me it doesn't sound like you were made to listen to a track of such timeless perfection. Go on, tell me. TESTIFY! Try and tell me that "Shot By Both Sides" doesn't harbor within it's turgid beat the greatest guitar riff in the post-punk canon.

That's right. You can't. 'Cause you know it's true. _Real Life_ is a bonafide classic, and pity to the woe-be-gones that can't recognize it.

Arty, filed under best of the rest...
I was of course aware of Magazine as a teenager, and the two most famous tracks here (no need to mention them by name!) would have been on my young radar... but alas Magazine did not enjoy any of my attention.

Subconsciously, I would have navigated towards more direct / simple indie / rock music of the age - see Buzzcocks, Skids, Xray Spex for example. Listening to this album for the first time in 2022 has however been something of an education.

I can hear so much herein that now resonates but this would have been too 'arty' for my teenage ears.

For sure Magazine's debut album can be a considered a classic of its era, and for me it now sits within the 'best of the rest category'.
