My god, I actually like this now. Everything but "Happy Family," especially the lovely oboe on "Bolero." Perhaps it is a little overbusy for my liking (which is why "Happy Family" is such a misfire), and the vocals are quite nondescript when compared to those of Greg Lake or John Wetton, but this is a unique album with moments of brilliant instrumentation, such as the horn trio on "Bolero," and Jon Anderson's guest spot on "Prince Rupert Awakes" is excellent. So I am now officially a part of Fripp's cult, I suppose. Which progger am I going to go for next? God, I hope it is not Peter Hammill or Styx guy.

Track picks: "Indoor Games," all of "Lizard."

John_s_Guide2Music Mar 28 2003
+5 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Maybe it's different when you know a record for over thirty years or when you discover an old record decades after its original release. I had lost track with the first four King Crimson LPs for some twenty years and incidentally re-discovered them. They are still my favorite King Crimson albums and the way I see it, one long suite consisting of 4 albums. Lizard used to be my favorite of these 4. Even after 30+ years, the music and the whole concept still sound not only fresh but daring - often surreal. I especially love the first three and the last two songs. There are so many details in here. There's Rock, there's Jazz, there's concert music, all amalgamated into something new - what more would I want? King Crimson ceased to dream up such magical soundscapes after Island. I hope the new listener can enjoy this LP the way I do, for I do realize there's also nostalgia involved in my judgment; yet the quality is still there, the LP has matured wonderfully.

After their fantastic "In The Wake of Poseidon" I expected the album to be the same quality as the previous one but I was disappointed.

Album starts with fantastic track "Circus" with Haskell's great vocals and great usage of saxophone. However next three tracks feel like filler.

"Indoor Games" and "Happy Family" sound like farts played through distorted trumpets with Haskell's vocals being destroyed by bad voice manipulation on the songs.
"Lady of the Dancing Water" truly feels like filler without a substance.

The titular track's first section "Prince Rupert Awakes" is the only good part of the song made by fantastic singing by Jon Anderson. Rest of "Lizard" is monotonous and not memorable.

_coldramen Feb 11 2024
Vote downBookmarkVote up
This is certainly more striking on subsequent listens. Theres a lot I like about this album now, I love the woodwinds all over and the horns play a larger role than they do in the typical schizoid man fashion. The vocals are nothing to write home about (except for the guess appearance by my man Jon Anderson) but the lyrics are sick, some of my favorite king crimson lyrics are on Happy Family and Lizard.

For King Crimsons only 20 minute song Lizard sure isnt an immediate stunner, its somewhat of a slow burn for me. The chorus is absolutely amazing, its moving in ways a lot of other Crimson parts arent for me.
I was definitely missing out the first time I listened to this, I like all the funny little quirks like the little synth line at the beginning of Indoor Games or the vocal effects on Happy Family.

Now I can appreciate the strange singularity of this album in the context of King Crimsons whole discography, it honestly matches up to the likes of In The Court, Red, and Discipline. Right after they tried to make the same album again this is a greatly unique album to hear.

First review from 1/9/23:
More forgettable than the previous two, and the vocals arent as great. Lizard starts off great with Jon Andersons amazing guest vocals but just kinda pitters off from there.
