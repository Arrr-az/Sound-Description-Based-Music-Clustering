Laughably overrated.

OH! I see why you guys circlejerk to this album!
Shrill Indie vocals that most likely influenced guys like Sufjan Stevens – CHECK! Acoustic guitar somewhere in every song no matter what? – CHECK! Horns and brass? Cuz you guys LOVE Horns n Brass – CHECK!
Honestly, though all those are true, this is a pretty good album. I can understand to a point, but honestly the love for this album confuses me more than the love for “Lift Your Skinny Fists” by Godspeed You! Black Emperor. I will say now, the first 3 songs ARE PERFECT, but then it gets to much and self-indulgent and just typical. Maybe this was the first Indie album that did all the tricks that every indie band uses now, but I’m too desensitized I supposed. Its still good, but not that special. “Two-Headed Boy Prt 1” is obnoxious as fuck and as pretentious as one can get with an acoustic guitar by far. Then “the Fool” is just throw away, as is “Communist Daughter” but more tolerable. “Holland, 1945” is really good though, almost as good as the first 3 songs. The ONLY thing I can give you with “Comely” is that for an extremely basic and unimpressive acoustic song, for 8 minutes it still holds up, that is pretty surprising, but that is it, stamina and no flash, woopdi doo I guess. “Ghost” is nice, its probably the only song where I see the intimacy and enjoyable feel that everyone gushes over besides just loving the setup of the first 3 songs (don’t you love how I never reference them by name as if I’m lying? I’m not, but what a stray observation). “Untitled” is awesome though, I don’t know why, but just 2 minutes of just slightly random folk with fuck tons of reverb. Holy shit I thought it was just my album, but apparently the reverb is on purpose, I will say that is the one original thing I hear on here, though again, this could be THE Indie album that started making Indie “cool to do”. This album really feels like it’s the origin of where modern hipsters have come from. If so… I don’t know if it’s a necessary evil to have. Good album – annoying fans – that’s everything though. I like it, but I’ll never talk about it with anyone, it will make me want to punch something or someone. And I don’t want to, because I’m sure everything within punching range when talking about this album is covered in jizz.

Dear RYM, I'm writing this confession to you because I have no one else to talk to. Maybe I do, but just don't really want or know how to. The aim is to tell you just how pathetic and miserable I am, and permanently it seems, although that wouldn't be very logical at all... doesn't time heal all wounds, as they usually tell us? I shall discover it once, but for the time being I seem as helpless as a leaf in the wind - I have no control over my life, my stupid emotions have overcome me to the point that I can hardly get out of bed in the morning. The reason is not just a bad breakup, it's a very special kind of breakup, with the only person I've ever met that I could imagine spending my life with. It actually happened a couple of weeks ago, but I felt its full aftermath today, after an unsuccessful attempt at reconciliation last night. So, here I am... waiting to hit rock bottom and then to hopefully bounce back to life. Because, as perversely as it may sound, I WANT to live.

I'm not ashamed to admit that I cried a lot this morning. In the Aeroplane Over the Sea was my soundtrack, just like a couple of times before. It is one of my two "crying" records par excellence (and if you knew which was the other one you'd probably disown me and delete my account, but please, if you ever find out, do not do it, for sometimes you're everything I have in this world), and if I know anything about anything it saved my life at least once before. I've also - this is actually shameful - googled for advice for the best ways to overcome a painful relationship break-up. They all make sense, but none of them is truly helpful. In the Aeroplane Over the Sea is, of course, not an album about relationship break-ups, but it at least justifies my quest for inner peace, which I've yet to find.

So here I am. At a crossroads. Again. Facing the future, looking back at the (very recent) past and barely living the unbearable present. It's like I'm waiting for that big change to come and make everything all right (just like the last one destroyed it all), calm things down and bring a certain ease into my existence. How, when, why and IF... all become irrelevant questions for they could never lead me anywhere... It's like an irrevocable mishmash of casual events and circumstances that seem to hover over me day after day, never knowing where does one end and the next one begins. The poking question is not SHOULD I be affected by a slightest little change of course in my unstable life (for, at least in theory, I know I shouldn't), but WHY am I? And will I ever be able to change anything about it... can one ever, and under what circumstances, change ones inner-self?

I fear that the answer is negative, but this record still gives me some comfort. It makes tomorrow seem all right, at least for a while. I love how Jeff, apparently, said that he'd never make another album because there was no sense in making a career out of this. In the same vein I could say that I will never refer to this relationship in my future writing again. The difference is that nobody would really care, for I am a mediocre "writer" and am sickeningly irrelevant in the grand scheme of things. But I'm also a coward, for I'd say so much more, but I can't. I'm not allowed. I need to restrain myself. It's for the better.

Still... I have decided to follow the path of Love, so perhaps I'll have nothing to fear. I'll start by saying that I love my ex, for giving me the cutest smile I'd ever seen when we met, for the undying sincerity and commitment, and for sharing with me both the body and the soul back then when we thought we had a promising future... I love you RYM, for letting me getting away with this scribbling, sharing my most intimate thoughts with a community of music lovers from all over the world, and not ridicule me in the process... I love my prof. C., the only human being who believed in me and encouraged me even when I was ready to give up on myself... I love my family, for all the missed opportunities we had and for all the support they've given me, even when they didn't know precisely how to articulate it... And I love You, Jesus Christ. I feel you today more than I ever did before. Please forgive me.

Neutral Milk Hotel singer Jeff Mangum composed music like "Yellow Submarine" and "It's All Too Much" were the Beatles' greatest, sang like Neil Young was the best a vocalist could be, and wrote lyrics as though directness was either toxic or deadingly blase, but on the marvelous In the Aeorplane Over the Sea he made one of the most magical albums of its time.  kaasprodukte Oct 02 2009
+5 Vote downBookmarkVote up
I recall some time ago that some whimsical bright spark posted a thread on the RYM forum asking (I am still not sure why?) people to post a picture of themselves holding a copy of their favourite album. Every third reply seem to be of some fellow lovingly cradling a copy of this album in their hands whilst pulling their very best ‘you know I’m cool now fuck off’ look.

Although I had heard the album previously, primarily due to my friendship with an independent music store owner, who used to play the bloody thing on loop seemingly every time I visited his store, I had never been stirred to get hold of a copy and pay it any serious attention, mainly just to annoy my mate who used to recoil in absolute horror when I would lie and say ‘this is shit’ just to satiate my need to be somewhat contrary.

But now the sight of a 1000 indie haircuts (there seemed to be no decipherable demographic link to those that posed in reply to the thread apart from a slight tendency towards leftfield haircuts) had piqued my interest and I downloaded the album.

Initially I so badly wanted to be able to say that the album is absolute pants and has just thrived on the whole ‘cult’ aspect that essentially entices those of a certain inclination to join their number with promises that they will enter the secret ‘kingdom of cooldom’…but to a large extent I cannot put my hand on my heart and say this is completely true without bowing completely to my need to be contrary, as there is a certain something to the album that I find most definitely alluring.

Perhaps it is the fact that the sound is without doubt extremely unique. Flashes of the conceptualism of The Decemberists spring instantly to mind, but it is just a bit to sparse to really be considered in the same vein. Certainly the insistent acoustic guitar strumming reminds me of a better version of The Woodentops and an inferior version of the better moments of The Feelies. All of the above is mixed with orchestral flourishes which revolve around the brass section that reminds me of some of the Sufjan Stevens stuff on Illinois (sorry cool kids)…mixed altogether it is certainly ‘not boring’ and has become a bit of a grower for me recently.

But surely ‘not boring’ could not persuade a million RYM-ers to join the ‘Neutral Cult’, there had to be something more to it than that, surely? As such I dug deep into the lyrics to find out what it was that made people listen to this on a Tuesday night when everyone else was vegging out in front of the telly…The lyrics were sure to hold the answer.

Well presumably I am not intelligent enough to find any real relevance in the lyrics, certainly not enough to explain the cult phenomenon. Essentially they seem to be a strange mix of stream of consciousness thoughts (think a John Fowles novel – but less boring) juxtaposed with regular references to dying, sex and the usage of the word semen in seemingly every track (semen as in ejaculation not The Decemberists recurring concept referring ‘boaty’ types).

I really hope it is my lack of intelligence which prevents me from finding any earth shattering ‘meaning’ to it all as the thought of the alternative is just too stomach churning to comprehend. Surely a million RYM-ers had not just gone all ‘Neutral’ because they saw it as cool? Could this be? Nah, I am just too dumb I suppose (hope) or perhaps I just have not concentrated enough after being put off by the goddamn awful vocal delivery which is excruciatingly poor whenever the track goes above mid tempo or the vocals are required to go beyond mid register.

In conclusion this is a decent listen but you will only enter the lofty ‘kingdom of cooldom’ via the wings of this aeroplane (do you see what I have done there – perhaps I am clever enough after all?) if you are ‘that way’ inclined. For the rest of us more grounded souls we will just have to accept the fact we will never be cool and that this album will always be fair to middling in our eyes.
