Like how New Order's "Technique" from '89 should've kept to the acid house...this album would have been *supreme* if they kept to the baggy-shoegaze fusion more. I'm aware that they would do so in later releases, but here...it needs it. Somehow I suspect it would've been their most mature work, but that's not what we're working with.

One of the tracks, "Pearl", being the most popular on the album and an example of the style...not to mention one of my favorites from it. "Falling Down" is another example.

"Autosleeper" is specifically a sort of "ambient shoegaze" and again one of my favorite tracks on here. Maybe my ears are particularly tuned into how compelling and great this truly sounds, because people seem to dismiss it as one of the weaker tracks here. And that's okay, after all it's your taste.

The rest is pretty much just run-of-the-mill shoegaze you would hear from the time period--the guitars do most of the wall of sound work.

While mostly a shoegazing affair, Whirlpool contains elements of the concurrent Madchester sound. It's an interesting album that finds some common ground between the two main styles of its era's British indie pop – that is, before all those ill-mannered youth were conscripted under the virtuous flag of Britpop. Chapterhouse's 1993 second and last album Blood Music would accentuate the dance floor elements but jettison shoegaze completely, resulting in a rather tepid brew of alternative dance and Britpop.
