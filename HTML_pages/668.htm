A frank opinion: the album charts for this decade on RYM are awful. The Top Ten for 2001, for example, is dominated by prog metal and metalcore albums that might appeal to metalheads but have generated very little enthusiasm from music critics or music fans in general. I mean, really, if the best that the music of this decade had to offer was Opeth or Tool's hard rock formulas, we really would have cause for despair.
And then there are wide discrepanices between the singles and the albums charts. New Slang by The Shins is rated close to #1 for 2001 but then this album, full of similar folky gems, languishes at #90. This is far from an isolated phenomenon and I am at a loss to explain why raters would like songs more when they appear on singles than albums. So to close another frank opinion: there were few better demonstrations of songwriting chops in the early 00s than this record.

BtC Mar 25 2010
+2 Vote downBookmarkVote up
When I first purchased this album it really was an immediate 5 star album, or if not very close to it. The subtle, sunny pop reminded me of something I'd listen to on a boat on a sunny, lazy day out on the lake waterskiing or something. It just shimmered and jangled right to the center of my heart.

What makes this album better than Chutes Too Narrow is the subtlety of the instruments. The sound is more full and in your face on CTN, and as a result loses much of the pretty nuances and janglepop qualities of their debut. Of course, I'm a lover of most things janglepop so this album fell right into my sweet spot.

Days have passed, and the initial lovefest has tempered a little, for whatever reason. So while I may not get outright giddy at the sublime vocal and guitar melodies of, say, "One by One All Day" or "Girl on the Wing" quite the way I used to, this is still a very solid album, and one of the best pop albums in my collection.

I do admit, I hate the way this album in particular was so closely attached to Garden State and that stupid "this song will change your life" line or whatever it was. It shouldn't bother me so much, but it does.

batfink Sep 04 2005
+2 Vote downBookmarkVote up
When they're parking the cars on your chest
You've still got a view of the summer sky...
-- "Know Your Onion"

Okay, Oh, Inverted World, so I finally get around to reviewing The Mighty One. While their follow-up album Chutes Too Narrow most certainly may be its equal, there is just nothing quite as amazing as re-living my initial encounter of The Shins' divine style. It was while combing over the university network in 2001 that I chanced upon an oddly out-of-place subfolder containing twelve mp3s, and it was through downloading them that I plunged deeply into the lair of Oh, Inverted World and the magical music of James Mercer & The Shins. A very pleasant and most fortunate accident.

Each time I progress through these tracks I fall back under the spell. The finesse of what are otherwise simple sounds, the beautifully executed art of a mood piece, it always grabs me. Those unrushed mellow sounds, I hazily drift along the dreamscape of The Shins' acoustic strumming and enveloping vocals. Sparkling songwriting is quite simply the key to this remarkably fresh sound, the pace changing subtley but wonderfully from song to song. Certainly my standout from the very start was always "New Slang", so enriching did I find the initial experience that I would collar my friends to force them to sit captive and listen to "this great new song man!" That fade up, those multi-layered various shades of mellow, that drowsy dtendu vocal matched up to the bright-yet-lazy strum... No song had sounded so great and so new to me in such a long time.

The rest of the album is also a wonderfully unfurrowed affair, soaring upwards from the opening seconds of "Caring Is Creepy", purring into "One By One All Day" with its spartan, mellow momentum and lightly-does-it guitar twangs. The lazy cantering of "Weird Divide" sounds like summer haze down on the riverbank (I think it's the shakers reminding me of cicadas), and "Know Your Onion" and "Girl Inform Me" are both great, sunny, happy songs, all swirling, jangling, and uplifting. After my favouritest-ever Shins song "New Slang" the quality never abates. "The Celibate Life" rolls into "Girl On The Wing" with its reverb piquing aplenty, blending sounds like some old 70s space music together into a pure of mellowy cruise. Then comes the ghostly brianwilsonesque "reverb-lite" of the little interlude of "Your Algebra", a caress-me-down effect to introduce the sparkling pick-me-up thump of "Pressed In A Book". It goes from great to greater in the finely performed acoustic croon "The Past And The Pending", leading to the final hazy track "Sphagnum Esplanade". Aaaaah...

Yes so I am quite biased towards this magnificent recording, how could you tell? But only rarely in my life has a new album from a new artist so compelled me, so deeply restored my faith and re-awoken my sensibilities to the limitless possibilities of simple musical instruments and their ability to express such a textured fabric of warm human emotion. Oh, Inverted World is a musical postcard of hope, an uplifting, oxygenating collection of amazing songs by a talented songwriter and will always remain for me a most special album.

Zephos Oct 29 2012
+1 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Who's up for some super melodic pop rock with a rawer edge to it? I don't know! Sometimes I am sometimes I'm not! I guess when you saw it all down that's kind of what stuff I really like like XTC and The Smiths is, but there's plenty of stuff I tend to find kind of eye rolling that can be described that way too. Of course thanks to the extremely useful label of "Indie Rock" I knew EXACTLY what to expect from the Shins when I started sniffing around 00's bands that people talked highly of. So I marched into this with the full confidence that I would definitely hear something alike Modest Mouse, TV on the Radio, and Liars! So what this really is, is a somewhat throwbacky glance toward melodic art pop of rock worlds past, but not so much of one as to not still be modern. In sharp contrast with lots of the bands stomping around the underground scene (insomuch as it actually is underground anymore) The Shins present a deliberately tuneful and even calming happy sound devoid of irony. Kurt Cobain's fuckin' corpse is spinning in it's fucking grave!!!! Ughhh!!! Oh thank god I say, there's great gloomy sadsacks marching around, but there's lots of tiring ones too. And ironic happy music is like...fuck. So this I welcome. At first I had to be sure I wasn't hearing a shameful outright throwback to 60's lite psychedelia, or like Donovan type stuff. And it does kind of feel like that here and there, but taking the whole album in you see that's not only a bit inaccurate...but doesn't even sum up the full scope of sound and vision here. It is a product of 2001, no more or less than Interpol is of next year. And what matters most of all is these fellas are really really good at writing melodies. Simple, unassuming, hugely satisfying, varying enough, and consistently. From nearly start to finish this just plain satisfies! With the noted exception of the dumb "Your Algebra" which needs to get cut out pronto once you own this. What that leaves though is a really high quality set of ten songs, Illmatic! Short albums in the post-vinyl era should truly be treasured you understand. Oh yeah, another thing, this is the Garden State thing, that Zach Braff movie that got mocked for INDIE MUSIK. I dunno, I never saw the movie and don't think I ever will. But I think the aversion to "indie musickk!!" some people have come to develop is nothing short of retarded. Firstly I think even buying into that whole distinction, where one album or another is underground or not is stupid. I don't care where it shot up to, what's it sound like? The attention spent to this? Generally it's the people who stick to underground who get labeled hipster, but fuck, if you turn your nose up at "underground" your as much of a hipster. My second thing is I saw so much of the 00's spent shitting on "indie rock" and hipsters. As if this was ever different. "No see, Animal Collective sucks! They're pretentious young elitists making purposely weird music! Sonic Youth? Now there was a great band!" Bitch please, if this was 1986 you'd be saying the exact same damn thing about Sonic Youth while praising the Velvet Underground. And if it were 1968? You get the picture. Maybe I'm this mad because as an impressionable teen I bought into so much of that negative hype and thought myself quite the "cool person" to be "above" boring indie twaddle no one ACTUALLY enjoyed. But of course how could you NOT enjoy this even a little bit? It's catchy, it's fun, it's easy, it's pretty. It's like...almost as if...the audience for this is people with ears??? Phew! Now now, I'm pretty sure that already 2001 is becoming slowly and surely "cool" instead of "UGHHH MODERN MUSIC" so that the anti-hipster hipsters are currently targeting Gotye or whatever and probably enjoying The Shins. But well, it's a timeless message that needs hearing.

The war cry of every slightly weird teenager who was unfortunate enough to grow up with Garden State as a focal cultural turning point for their taste. Amazing how tepid and unadventurous this sounds now, quirky but empty, interested in cultivating a retro-modern folk sound but too afraid to give into itself completely, James Mercer's brittle vocals whisper-shouting over clacking percussion and faltering hooks. The cultish hymn that is "New Slang" has mostly withstood the test of time but everything else feels outdated, disengaged and meandering from one idea to the next. Then again I pretended these guys were the Beatles of indie rock so who's the bigger loser here.
