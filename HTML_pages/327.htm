Two of the greatest works of King Crimson are slightly weakened by songs like Moonchild (In the Court of the Crimson King) and Providence (Red) as far as listening experience goes. Here we have none of that, the weird song is just great and atmospheric, it even samples their namesake song towards the end with the a-a-a-a-ah part, offering a nice touch to an epic song (with parts written by Gustav Holst: I'm talking about The Devil's Triangle, in fact it's a Holst rewriting, I'm avoiding the term 'cover'). That said, this album is the little brother of the debut. The songs can be identified by, "this is the Epitaph" (title-track here), "this is the 21st Century" (Pictures of a City), "this is the I Talk to the Wind" (Cadence and Cascade) of the album, and so on and so on.

So by originality and influence alone In the Wake of Poseidon is inferior to In the Court of the Crimson King and Red as well (my other favourite one), while being stylistically a direct sound-alike to In the Court.

That said, the three-part Peace is greatly atmospheric, it gives a backbone to the album (intro, intermezzo, outro), the title-track is amazing and the others also come close, especially the Emerson, Lake & Palmer-like Cat Food (no wonder, it's Greg Lake here on vocals) and the aforementioned Pictures of a City.

Try the band's more important albums first, but never forget that this is a classic that stands its own ground, too

Blatant self-plagiarism.

finulanu Jan 25 2009

Okay, so Crimson changed the game with their debut. We all know that. In the Court of the Crimson King was the first progressive rock album I can listen and still keep my lunch down. And hey, it was a good album! I'm no prog fan by any stretch, and I definitely don't love King Crimson as much as everyone else on this site does, but I know good music when I hear it, and that is good music. However, the Crim wasted no time in proving that their follow-up would basically be the exact same thing as that album.

I'm guessing it was because they were still reeling from all those lineup changes. Ya see, the entire band got shuffled around after Court. Greg Lake left to become the "Lake" in Emerson, Lake and Palmer, who are of course one of the worst bands in the history of music, although he at least stuck around to sing on most of the songs here, but he didn't play bass - that was left up to Peter Giles, formerly of Giles, Giles, and Fripp. Ian MacDonald also jumped ship, and kicked around for a few years before joining Foreigner, who are also one of the worst bands on the planet. Since bass never was a huge part of Crimson's sound, a new bassist could be slipped in easily without anyone noticing, although it would come at the expense of Lake's powerful vocals, but it would come at the expense of MacDonald's multifacted talents - the guy played a huge array of woodwinds and mellotron on the first record. Fripp tried to fill the gap by taking up mellotron himself and hiring session man Mel Collins to play horns, but neither of them can do anything near as interesting as what MacDonald did with those instruments. So that's a problem.

The bigger problem, though, is how eerily this follows the structure of its far better predecessor. Now, the good news is, there is no "Moonchild" equivalent - taking up its slot is the decent but rather annoying "Cat Food," which I think was a small hit or something but the bad news is, this is what the debut would sound like with all of the soul and creativity sucked out of it. It's sort of freaky how perfectly this follows that album's lead. If we take out the "Peace" thingy that runs throughout, it starts off with a dissonant jazz rocker ("Pictures of a City" vs. "21st Century Schizoid Man"), and follows it up with a restrained, pretty ballad, because hey, contrast is fun - this would be "Cadence and Cascade" to "I Talk to the Wind." From there, you get a long, mellotron-drenched epic with a dramatic chorus and a big ending (title track here; "Epitaph" there), and finally, to close things off, an epic that moves through a mind-boggling number of parts - the only discernible difference between "The Devil's Triangle" and "In the Court of the Crimson King" is that "Devil's Triangle" has no lyrics. And sucks. I mean really, really sucks. Thankfully, it's the only one.

Unfortunately, there's nothing here that will make you forget the debut, either, and I can't see any reason for anyone to listen to this album knowing that one exists. "Cadence and Cascade" is a rather pretty ballad and is easily the best song here, but other than that, the only entertaining bits I find are the pretty classical guitar playing in "Peace - A Theme" and the ending to "In the Wake of Poseidon," which is sort of interesting in how eerily similar it is to the ending of "Epitaph," right down to the drum fills. And this sort of gets me thinking. Wasn't anyone involved here aware of and annoyed by the blatant self-thievery? Well, even if they weren't, I am. And I call bullshit. But listenable bullshit.

On a lighter note, I have now listened to every album I gave the "needs further investigation" tag to back in October! This brings me much closer to achieving my goal of rating everything in my collection! Mission: almost accomplished.

Track picks: "Cadence and Cascade," sort of.

King Crimson do it again! But, wait, this band isn't supposed to "do it again"...

When they try to replicate the material from the debut, they fall flat on their face. "Pictures of a City" and "In the Wake of Poseidon" are flawed facsimiles of superior tracks. Bizarrely, while the 1969 songs still sound timeless, the 1970 ones sound oddly dated. Weird. And not good at all.

When they do something different, we get mixed result. "The Devil's Triangle" does nothing for me and it goes on for far too long. I'll give them points for throwing in a random snippet of the "In the Court of the Crimson King" chant. Funny stuff. Also, the "Peace" trilogy is dull filler. Yeah, I said it.

But there are times when they succeed, and brilliantly so! "Cadence and Cascade" is beautiful little ballad, featuring some remarkably symmetrical compositional characteristics as well as pretty vocals from Gordon Haskell. He's no Greg Lake, but his brittle voice makes the piece more approachable. Oh, and "Cat Food" is AMAZING. Zonky and weird and jazzy and off-kilter and psyched out... Man, I love that track!!!!!!

If you liked the debut and wish for more, then try this. If you liked the debut and like King Crimson's innovative tendencies, avoid this. If you're a completist, you'll need this. Otherwise, hell, just get it for "Cat Food" and "Cadence and Cascade."

Note: Be sure to avoid the 1989 Definitive Edition remaster. This series is the dictionary definition of flat sounding music. Get the 30th Anniversary Edition, since that one has quality sound and two bonus tracks (the single version of "Cat Food" and "Groon," the b-side).

Ms que una sombra
El segundo lbum de estudio de King crimson es... Raro, despus de la partida de Ian mcdonald y del xito de su anterior lbum trataron de replicarlo pero a da de hoy es recordado como poco menos que una sombra de su primer lbum. An as pienso que tiene buenas ideas y es un lbum bastante decente, no llega al nivel de su primera obra pero merece ser tratado con respeto

El lbum empieza con Peace - A Beginning y Pictures of a city, ambas canciones sirviendo como una introduccin que recuerda bastante a 21 Century schizoid man en estructura, aunque no se limita a ser un calco de esa cancin, teniendo su propio color y estilo propio haciendo uso de una guitarra acstica, unas lneas de bajo y solos de saxophon que le dan un aura diferente a la cancin, es bastante gil y divertida de escuchar pero sin el frenetismo de 21 Century , es una cancin con su propio color y estilo. A partir de aqu viene lo que considero la peor parte del lbum, cadance and cascade e In the wake of poseidon, ambas canciones son intentos desesperados de repetir I talk to the wind y epitaf pero sin la misma fuerza de esas canciones, no son malas exactamente pero se nota que son intentos baratos de replicar su anterior lbum, son canciones aburridas y que lastran el lbum.
A partir de aqu la cosa mejora con un pequeo Interludio instrumental como continuacin a la primera cancin, Peace- a theme, es un interludio sencillo pero al menos tiene una buena meloda de guitarra acstica que quita un poco el mal sabor de boca de las 2 anteriores canciones, a esta le sigue probablemente la mejor cancin del lbum, cat food, es un temazo bastante gil que recuerda a la energa de a Picture of a city, es bastante divertida de escuchar y recuerda bastante al rock psicodelico que se hara en estados unidos ms tarde en la dcada del 70, es una gran cancin que en cierto modo estaba adelantanda a su poca.

Por ltimo, The devils triangle y el outro, Peace- An end. The devils triangle es una cancin dividida en 3 partes que va aumentando su intensidad con el tiempo , es una cancin muy interesante que usa notas de piano atonales para darle un aura de urgencia a la cancin, es un tema bastante intenso. El lbum cierra con Peace- An end que es una fusin entre los 2 anteriores interludios, me parece un buen final que constrasta con el final de su primer, In the court of the crimson king terminaba de forma abrupta y violenta con un silencio amenazante que te dejaba pensando en lo que habas experimentado, pero este se siente diferente, es una cancin con una instrumentacin sencilla con una letra que habla sobre el significado de la paz, es un final esperanzador en contra del nihilismo del anterior lbum, puede que sea una lbum imperfecto pero tiene corazn y buenas ideas, se merece algo ms que ser solo una sombra
