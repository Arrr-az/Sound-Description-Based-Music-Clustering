Everything Youve Come To Expect is everything The Age of the Understatement wishes it was, its a lush record with beautiful ornate string sections mixed in with fantastic indie rock stylings topped off with such a strangely psychedelic and sexy feeling to it all. Miles Kane and Alex Turners weird poetic songwriting is implemented on here in such an excellent way, so many expertly written tracks on par with the best of Arctic Monkeys (at least in terms of songwriting) like the title track, Miracle Aligner, Aviation, The Dream Synopsis and Sweet Dreams, TN which is the best song I think Alex Turner has ever written. Theres something so irresistible about the words in this track, the way he speaks about his strange obsession with his love interest is so timeless and resonates so close to home. From a composition standpoint, this record is absolutely lovely. The string arrangements are so lush and pretty and compliment the bands baroque pop/indie rock instrumentation so perfectly, creating a sound that feels so timeless. Songs like opener Aviation, Miracle Aligner, Dracula Teeth, the title track, Pattern and The Element Of Surprise are so incredibly catchy and use their rhythm and guitars so well, and the powerful climax of Sweet Dreams, TN is one of my favourite moments in all of music. Fantastic record through and through, I cant wait for the trilogy to be completed.

Everything Youve Come to Expect is another step forward for Alex Turner, aka. front man of Arctic Monkeys, creating more downtempo music. It revolves around a retro aesthetic that is absent in any Arctic Monkeys albums. And its end product can be described as melodic, soothing and sluggish.

The album starts off with a fairly upbeat song Aviation, featuring Miles Kane as the lead vocals and a more noticeable bassline. Miracle Aligner offers a better picture of what the albums about, as Alex melts into the dreamy-retro atmosphere layered with acoustic guitars and beautiful violins. The song climaxes with keyboards dancing around, until the last chord is played dramatically, signifying its end. Dracula Teeth continues where Miracle Aligner has left off, but with a haunting undertone. This mood is also evoked in lyrics such as I wake up in an ice-cold sweat, and my skin starts to creep and the full moons glowing yellow, and the floorboards creek. Alex is right, cest horrifque!

While titled track Everything Youve Come To Expect is not as dark as the previous tracks, it seems as if the song is crawling to escape some sort of danger with its slow tempo. The tension built is resolved in The Element Of Surprise with a playful manner. It doesnt shine as much as the rest of the album, which is quite ironic for its title. Unless it is referring to the next track, Bad Habits. Yes, it always surprises me, how did anyone let this end up on the album? Not only it doesnt vibe with the overall mood of the album, it is just stupid.

Sweet Dreams, TN returns with a majestic sound. Unlike the rest of the songs, it is more vocally driven and emotional. The lyrics are no longer used to create an ideal mood, but to declare Alexs love for his ex girlfriend, making it more substantial and direct. It is also notable that Sweet Dreams, TN is the only track with lyrics solely credited to Alex. Though some might find it cheesy for the countless babys in the first verse, I find it amusing.

Used To Be My Girl is the moment of cockiness and dirtiness. She Does The Woods is repetitive.

Pattern brings back its melodic approach, slipping and sliding all the way with a sense of sorrow. The haunting backing vocals just alleviates the whole experience, which is one of the reasons why this still remains to be my favourite track on EYCTE. The album closes off beautifully with ballad The Dream Synopsis, dealing with lonesome and nostalgia.

The Bourne Identity is an additional track, but it is one of the most authentic Alex Turner has ever been in his songwriting, talking about his self hatred. He has been known for changing looks to fit each albums, but he confesses how it causes him to loose his sense of identity with yeah Im the mystery man thats been unmasked, Im getting caught in my own trap. Sobs.

The album that, for me, confirms Alex Turner as the song writer of a generation. Not that Miles Kane isn't equally impressive; just listen to his voice on 'Pattern', so perfectly melodic against the foreboding tone of those strings and guitars.

Everything You've Come To Expect is flawless, one of those records where everything slots in to place, drenched in self-absorbed love for its bluesy neo-60s sound and its tales of romantic escapades. The sultriness is as attractive as Kane and Turner are in their short-sleeved shirts and Clubmasters in the accompanying artwork.

Some find it too snobby; I find the confidence in its own arrogance all the more reason to love it, and when it shows its heart on 'The Dream Synopsis', nothing else really compares.

"Seduced by luxury to the sounds of a string quartet"
By the time that Everything You've Come to Expect came to fruition, Alex Turner was no stranger to experimentation. Already five albums deep into the monumental indie rock phenomenon that Arctic Monkeys had become at this stage, the band's frontman was hot off the heels of a worldwide tour and his most successful album yet. He had explored vibrant jangle pop in 2011's Suck It and See and mellow folk performances on his solo soundtrack to the coming-of-age film Submarine, and had overall become the epitome of musical stardom in the mid-2010s. And while it's not as much a left turn as the space-themed Tranquility Base Hotel & Casino that had yet to become a reality, Turner's second full collaboration with Miles Kane as The Last Shadow Puppets is a sophisticated exhibition of splendor and fame.

The orchestra-backed tracklist to this album races along like a series of modern James Bond theme tunes. It brings a whole new meaning to lavishness, a rich grandiloquence that is best exemplified in the music video for the tremendous single Miracle Aligner. First set up as a foreign dramatic romance picture, the miniature film then bursts into a portrayal of a spray-tanned Turner and Kane prancing through a golden ballroom in slow motion, their flowing suits and synchronized dance moves oozing with an almost royal exuberance. The thrilling atmosphere between the musicians' close encounters and flamboyant choreography undoubtedly sparked a countless number of Tumblr posts and fanfiction write-ups, making the revisitation of these songs and videos a trip into a different era of internet culture altogether.

Everything You've Come to Expect is a high-budget crossover between the most ridiculous spaghetti western, a gimmicky secret agent flick, and a steamy rom-com. Fans of AM's maximal production will be more than pleased with this record's forays into psychedelic territory, and it's one more achievement for Alex Turner's sensational output in a decade where he was practically a king.

Jet-set music
Alex Turner, yes! But stylistically, aeons away from Arctic Monkeys being more 'chamber pop', 'psychedelic pop' and melodic 'pop / rock'-orientated.
Nothing negative about that as such. The album has generally been met by positive reviews by... most major critics.
I just don't find it that interesting. Admitted, it's above mediocre but there are too few fine songs here to call it anything near great. The production side is fine - very neat and polished - but the songs are just too anonymous and soothing in big arrangement production for my liking. And then I just find that it builds a bit too much on The Divine Comedy and former heydays with Scott Walker and Lee Hazlewood.
It's like Alex Turner going with the jet-set.
