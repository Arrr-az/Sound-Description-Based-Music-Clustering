I find most of CCR's albums to be interchangeable (great, but interchangeable), particularly the run from Bayou Country to Cosmo's Factory. This isn't meant to be a slight - they're all great mixes of massive, iconic singles that never seem to grow old, and lesser-known album cuts - but I'll be damned if I can remember offhand which one of them houses "Run Through the Jungle," or "Travelin' Band," or "Proud Mary" (though I'm pretty sure "Green River" is on Green River).

Pendulum is different. It seems way more cohesive to these ears, like a concerted effort to really define the band's sound. "Hey Tonight" and "Have You Ever Seen the Rain" stick out of course, but both are symptomatic of the shift towards the band trying to sound less like they just fell off the back of a moonshine wagon, and more like a band that's actually from San Francisco. It's a more mature, more soulful record, and perhaps a last gasp at harmony before the shit hit the fan.

Check out the more well-known records first if you haven't already. This underrated piece will be here when you get back.

My Favorite 5: 1) Have You Ever Seen the Rain 2) (Wish I Could) Hideaway 3) Hey Tonight 4) It's Just a Thought 5) Born to Move

Least Favorite: Rude Awakening #2


If you scan the RYM user ratings for CCR's first six albums, you'll notice Pendulum is rated significantly lower than the others, and much lower than the three that immediately preceded it. Why? There's loads of good stuff here, and I enjoy the mixture of rough 'n' tumble rock 'n' rollers ("Pagan Baby," "Chameleon," "Born to Move," "Hey Tonight," "Molina") and gentler stuff ("Sailor's Lament," "Have You Ever Seen Rain," "(Wish I Could) Hideaway," "It's Just a Thought").

(Note: Right in the middle of my writing this review (after I had just written "Why?"), the American men's swim team won the gold medal in the 4 x 100m freestyle relay at the 2008 Beijing Summer Olympics by overtaking France in the last leg. The favored French team was ahead by what looked like an insurmountable distance--and the USA appeared to be cruising for the silver--when Jason Lezak exploded down the stretch and caught the French anchorman right at the wall, setting the world record for fastest split in the 4 x 100, as well as the world record for his team in the relay itself. I'm convinced my cheering and cavorting and frantic hand motions caused this victory for the Americans. The French team had made some brazen comments about how they were going to "smash" the Americans, making the loss very painful for them and very sweet for team USA, I'm sure.)

Catalog Crawl (#7)
Tom Fogerty's last album with CCR doesn't quite manage to capture the magic of the band's previous five records, but Pendulum remains a dark horse fan-favorite regardless. Almost-certainly reflecting the inner-group turmoil that would lead to the elder Fogerty's looming departure, Pendulum is defined by a notably wearier sound — one augmented by the uncharacteristically heavy presence of keyboards.

Album centerpiece, "Have You Ever Seen the Rain," stands as John Fogerty's last truly untouchable Creedence composition; and its upbeat B-side, "Hey Tonight," is another clear highlight. Elsewhere, Pendulum is dotted with tracks that tend to take longer than usual to reveal their charms, but which reveal their charms nevertheless. It's all capped off by "Rude Awakening #2": a cryptic and experimental instrumental that is arguably the most divisive — and certainly one of the more fascinating — tracks in the band's discography.

Rating: 7.6
