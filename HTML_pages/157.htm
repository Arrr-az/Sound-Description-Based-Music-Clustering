Odd_even Nov 04 2009  
+8 Vote downBookmarkVote up
It's documented that Gal Costa's mother, while pregnant with the angelic voiced child, listened to classical music with the intention of getting that sweet little bubba in her womb interested in music. Yes, I stole that from wikipedia right there, but Jesus Christ is worked like a charm didn't it. And where does one else start when describing the music of Gal Costa? It's just something so special, the overall blend of all the elements, the instruments, the production, her voice...it's a galaxy of its own. She's always been involved with music through some network of links or means; befriending Gilberto Gil and Caetano Veloso future wives, working in a record store before finally getting a concert appearance alongside the likes of Gil and Veloso. And so it was from therein, she was a household name in the MPB scene.

There is something just so captivating about her music though. The tropical flavour of the guitar, whether it be static of gentle strummed, the bossa-nova hi-hat tapping and occasional sax intrusion, the bongos patted gently yet briskly; and above all, her soft voice wrapping all these elements up in a quilt of feathers and elevating them off into her own universe in the softest and most majestic of manners. We have a vast range of tempos and styles, but Costa breezes through each song with an air of elegance and confidence. She knows what she is doing. She is not one to fuck with. We have a duet with Caetano Veloso which is a compelling charm; however the most notable moment for one such as myself is the cover of "Baby", which in my opinion is the definitive version of the famous song among her other contemporaries of Brazil. Costa just simply has the perfect voice for a song of such tenderness. You just sink into her voice, so soothing, so enticing; it's hard to resist temptation. Gone were the days were Costa was soothing the wide-eyed patrons at a humid Brazilian club; no, she was now world renowned. And rightfully so.

lanky_caravan Apr 17 2009

Simply the most brilliant album I've heard in a long, long time - and now it is mine thanks to Taz Records on Argyle and Four Men with Beards!

I prefer the debut over her harder edge Gal with Cinema Olympic. The pop bug bit her hard on this album, I find it all listenable with more than a little bit of psychedelic experimentation on it to set my mind at ease.

According to my itunes, Divino Maravilhoso is my most played track of all time. Heretofore I was running a copy I downloaded. My dad thinks the whole torrent thing is immoral and ungodly, but how on earth would I have found Gal without rym and the net. I told him that if I really like something, I will buy it. To which he replied, "Why?" Didn't even want to explain to him how much better a listening experience it is to hear it on vinyl, especially on a release like this where every part (drums and percussion, bass, electric guitars, b. vocs) is important. I feel like I support internet downloading because I've paid (heavily) to join the club of legal music purchasing, so shouldn't I have the right to know what it is I am shopping for!

Anyway, this is hardly the stuff for a review, and I don't mean to offend my Gal by disrepect. Gal Costa is so divine. I think I have a crush on her circa 1968. Her taste in material is impeccable. The opener is devasting, a hauntingly beautiful ballad, while the closer by Jorge Ben is one of the bounciest endings I've ever heard. Also love this version of Baby with Caetano gently croonin' Paul Anka's Donna at the end. Sebastian, with what amg refers to as vocals "that sound like they are recorded at the bottom of a well," gets me so pumped for the rest of side a, which mysterious floats along according to its own logic. Don't understand a word, tried babelfishing this shit to death but I feel no closer to knowledge except for the english versions of Baby. "Oh well," say me and Gal, at the bottom of our well...

This could be in my top ten

The Debut Album Project: Day 264

Though it arrived just beyond the pivotal year of 1968, Gal Costa’s solo debut is one of the definitive documents of Brazil’s vibrant Tropicalia scene. Featuring compositions and guest spots from many of the movement’s leading lights (Gilberto Gil, Caetano Veloso, and Tom Zé), Gal Costa is a whirlwind of rhythmic and melodic abundance. And on the subject of those Brazilian titans, this LP is easily on par with any of their highly acclaimed debuts.

No matter how eclectic it is — and it’s pretty damn eclectic — there is an undeniable warmth that flows throughout this album: one that provides more than enough of a unifying quality. But never once does it come off as showy, or anything less than wholly natural. Rather, this is an artist firmly within her element, and firmly in control of her considerable artistic gifts.

And because of the aforementioned eclecticism, I could provide a host of reasonable reference points. However, you’re best off just listening to the damn thing, because it’s a stone cold, front-to-back fucking classic.
