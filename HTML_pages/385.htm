I think that my least favourite noise in the world is the sound of a fax machine that won't connect to another fax machine. Especially when it keeps redialling the other machine with no results other than to repeat the painfully annoying sound ad infinitum.

Obviously I haven't heard noises such as thousands of starving children moaning as they die, or the snap of a condemned man's neck as he hangs himself alone in his cell, nor a tree falling in the woods with nobody there to hear it, except for me, hidden just out of sight so as to not technically be there as far as the tree is concerned. How does a tree fall anyway without someone or something being around to saw it or knock it down? Lightning is one explanation I suppose, but many trees have trunks with diameters that exceed the size of my flat so we can't use this as an explanation for every instance of a tree falling alone and unaccompanied by living, hearing things, in a forest.

If I had heard those noises, then fax machines failing to connect to one another may well be further down the least pleasant list, but as things stand it rests safely in pole position.

The other day in the office this noise kept on repeating itself until I was forced to reach for some form of drug, and it just so happened that Spiritualized's Ladies and Gentleman, We Are Floating in Space was at hand, sticking out of it's packaging ever so slightly, tempting me with it's easy accessibility. I yanked it from the package and swallowed the tablet whole, using only the spit in my mouth as lubricant with which to wash it down my neck.

Almost immediately I realised what a massive mistake I had made, the damned packaging was so clever that it had fooled me into eating an actual album. Horrified, I slumped into a chair. The impact caused some unusual whirring noises to emanate from my stomach and I remembered that I was in luck; earlier in the week I had swallowed a portable CD player after being lulled into thinking it was a joint of beef by, again, incredibly clever packaging. It seemed that the two accidents had resulted in a happy marriage and We Are Floating in Space began playing away inside of me.

I had to remain seated throughout the experience as I felt sedated, and there were certainly some mind altering effects raging in the contortions of my bowels, but the music was so absorbing that I no longer heard or cared about the fax machine beeping away in the corner of the room.

I cried from time to time, particularly when the record began as the pain of the CD player in my stomach jerking into action mid decomposition combined with the heart breaking melodies which sounded like they were lifted straight from Pachelbell's Canon was practically overwhelming. At other times I laughed and my feet twitched and my heart and soul revelled in the pure elation of musical layers working together, and I mused at the dark/beautiful patchwork of the lyrics and got all goose-pimply over the Gospel choirs.

It was a great experience, made better by the fact that, by the time it was over, the fax machine had connected to its source and was now sitting silently in the corner of the room, its single red eye blinking away in the shadows.

My only concern now was the inevitable finale of my most fortunate accident, but I remembered that I had never been too phased by the burning exiting of jalapeno peppers, so the final movement of the CD player should be a walk in the woods. Maybe I would hear a tree falling...

TheCunningStunt Jan 20 2012  
+21 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating in Space is Spiritualized's masterpiece. Nothing else even comes close to it, an album that only comes along once in a while but when it does come along, you have to embrace it. The best of their career, and the best of its genre. It's a truly special album.

Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating in Space is an album I've loved for a long time, but I think I love it now more than ever, it never gets boring because there's so much to it. Every time I listen to it, I notice little intricacies that I previously missed and my attachment to it means I'll hold it very dear to my heart. Now, I'm going to talk about my attachment to it for the rest of this paragraph, I'm going to go into anecdote mode telling you why the album means so much to me. If you're not interested and you want to actually hear about the album itself, then skip to the next paragraph. But reading why it means so much to me may give you more of an understanding of what this album's really about. So... When I was 16, I was happy. At least, I was happier than I'd ever been in my life. Looking back, I think I've always been a bit of a miserable cunt. I'm not sure why, but I've always had this lingering sadness and it's gradually increased as I've got older. It's more of an unmitigated sadness, and it makes me ask the question "Do I listen to pop music because I'm miserable or am I miserable because listen to pop music?" The way I am is something that I've almost always hidden from my family and friends, but then I found some happiness. School made me incredibly unhappy, I hated every single day of it, I couldn't relate to anyone there and I felt an awful lot of bitterness and resentment. But when I turned 16, it was finally over. I've never been much of a people person, I don't tend to like many people, but I actually found a couple of friends that I held close to me and to top it off, I fell in love for the first time. Fast forward a few months and my first proper relationship was disintegrating. I was in college, I was 17 and I did what any 17 year old male would do - I drank myself stupid. The weird thing is, I always think of this album as being the soundtrack to the event which is really odd because back then, I wasn't as interested in music. I liked a few bands - The Smiths, Oasis, The Stone Roses and a few others but I found this album and I couldn't stop listening to it but I have no idea how, why or where I found it. It didn't really fit in with the other bands I loved at the time, but I absolutely adored it. If you don't know, this album is a break up album. I always think that there's something really special about break up albums. So when I listened to it, I immediately connected to it and it helped me through a tough time. Cool story brah.

Even though I love it all, there's one track that I'd take over any other. 'Stay With Me', I mean, how could you not relate to it? It's about that moment where you want to just lie with her and for that moment to last forever. The lyrics, despite being simplistic, are incredibly affecting and it makes for the most genuinely beautiful moment on the album. "Oh babe, I love the way you slide, stay with me. I love the way you're mine, don't go." Jason Pierce's vocals are just so desperate, and you can just hear in his voice that it's over and that she's slipping away and it's a song about just wanting to hold on to her. Nothing more, nothing less. One of the most human songs on the album, but there's so many genuinely beautiful moments. From 'All Of My Thoughts' "Don't know how to sleep on my own, 'cause all of my dreams are with you. I just don't know what to do on my own, 'cause all of my thoughts are of you'." Which just describes that first moment of loneliness so well to 'Home Of The Brave' - "I don't even miss you but that's cause I'm fucked up and sure when it wears off then I will be hurting" which describes being so messed up on whatever substances you're having, that you just don't feel it any more, in my case it was alcohol but I'm sure in Jason Pierce's case it was heroin. But that's the wonderful thing about music, you can relate to it in whatever way you choose.

That period of my life, it all seems a lot more insignificant now. But when it was happening, it was the most real thing to ever happen to me. Not just the falling in love part and the girl herself, but everything that went with the whole leaving school going into college phase. It was the most important year and a half of my life, but in the end, it doesn't really mean all that much. But if I'm lucky enough to get fall in love, get married, have children and feel content, fulfilled and truly happy for the first time in my life. The moment I get divorced and all that gets taken away from me, you can bet I'll listen to this album.

Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating In Space is dreamy, soaring, powerful, heartbreaking and sincere. One of the most visceral and honest albums I've ever listened to. You've heard my story and why I love the album, but if you don't already know, the album was made because as I mentioned - it's a break up album with a twist. Jason Pierce loved Kate Radley, Kate Radley secretly married Richard Ashcroft, Jason Pierce was then left heartbroken and he turned to heavy substance abuse. You have all the emotion, pain and anguish of a break up album combined with all the trippiness and weirdness of drugs - as well as the talent of the band to create beautiful, complex, layered music that has amazing songwriting behind it. The way the layers and layers of instruments just keep building up until they culminate in a noisy crescendo never fails to captivate me.

Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating In Space is absolutely essential, but quite hard to listen to at times.
