By this time, Roger Waters was beginning to reap a lot of criticism for his artistic ego, but he'd never really told his story until The Wall - through the eyes of an Aquarius Age playwright. Virtually every knock against this album is accurate. But the lingering genius is how the band came up with so many tracks that will eternally stand up as individual statements, thanks to classic rock radio (which draws in new buyers to begin with).

The themes here are undoubtedly important (fascism, repression, abuse, war, loss, narcotic escape, etc.), yet who would want to hear a ninety-minute song over and over again? But the Floyd had learned a few things from their last album, the highly uncommercial Animals. Most of the hits all have their own self-contained sub-themes and compositional economy. I'd love to scale this album down to a single lp just to see what would happen. Wouldn't you just love a side one that had "Young Lust," "Hey You," "ABITW2," and "Mother?" And a side two that had "In the Flesh," and finished with "Comfortably Numb?" Sure, "Waiting for the Worms" and "The Trial" are good songs only Floyd could have done, but then you have to let in the other ten cuts to flesh out the story to Roger's satisfaction.

Bloated (yet flat) in spots, but still a major artistic and intellectual achievement; except when compared to its own ambition. And rock operas were already a decade past their sell-by date.

The 1979 release The Wall is a controversial masterpiece written by Pink Floyds bassist Roger Waters released as an official Pink Floyd album. The album is a concept album or a rock opera, which tells the story of Pink.

When I listened the album for the first time I hated the whole album, with the exception of Comfortanly Numb, which I immediately liked and which still is one of my all time favorite songs. But I listened the album again and again and with every spin I liked it more and little by little The Wall became one of my favorite albums of all time.

The album tells the story of Pink, a young man and rock star, whose father died in the Second World War. His mother tried to replace the father and tried to hide her son from everything bad in the world. He didnt liked school and his teacher was sadistic and didnt support his creativity and his individuality.
He was married but as a rock star he had affairs and his wife left him. All this things were the reason that Pink built an imaginary Wall to save himself from everything in the world, to isolate himself from any feelings. Once the Wall was built Pink desperately is looking for contacts. He isnt able to tear down the Wall and becomes a drug addict. He doesnt want to play concerts anymore, because he feels like a machine. His drug addiction becomes worse and Pink is in a delirium and thinks he is haunted and then later sees himself as a leader of a fascistic organization.
At the end of the album Pink isnt able to repress all his feelings, so he accuses himself for having showed feelings. The imaginary jury found him guilty and tells him to finally tear down the Wall, he built to isolate himself from any form of feelings.

The album was entirely written by bassist Roger Waters and some of the parts are autobiographical. For example Roger Waters father died also in the Second World War. The lost of his father is the main topic of many songs, that Roger Waters has written in his career for Pink Floyd or for solo projects. The Wall was the Roger Waters project and according to the other band members Roger Waters like a dictator and told everyone what he had to do, because he had a fixed idea of the album in his mind. The fact that Roger Waters wanted to control everything was the main reason for the split of the classic Pink Floyd lineup and the fact that the thing that Roger Waters wanted the rights for the album The Wall, when Pink Floyd went to court to clear who has the rights to perform under the banner of Pink Floyd, proves how important this album was and still is for Roger Waters, it is his own Magnus Opus.

Musically the concept is transcribed well by the band. There are many changes of the mood that are equal to the mood of Pink. The contrast between the aggressive voice of Roger Waters and the soft voice of David Gilmour is as great as the change between soft acoustic guitar or piano parts and heavy electric guitar dominated parts. Its hard to pick a song, because the album is total work of art that works in its entirety but there are some songs I want to point out. As I said my favorite track is Comfortably Numb, which feature a great guitar solo by David Gilmour, on of the best guitar solos of all time. Other great tracks are the wonderful acoustic pieces Mother and Hey You, the rocking tunes Young Lust, Run Like Hell and the two songs In the Flesh? and In the Flesh!. The most famous song is of course the hit single Another Brick in the Wall Part 2, which was the best selling Pink Floyd song of all times along with Money of The Dark Side of the Moon.

The album was performed live in its entirety in London, Los Angeles, Dortmund and New York in huge arenas. Roger Waters created a huge live show, which could only be performed in big arenas, so the band performed 5 or 6 concerts in one city and then went on to next arena. The main attraction of the show was a wall that was built and hided the band from the crowd and that was torn down at the end of every show. Also huge puppets, lasers and other effects were part of this show.
The concerts of The Wall tour were the last concerts performed by the classic lineup not including the short reunion for the live aid show in 2005 but Roger Waters performed The Wall again.

He played a legendary concert in Berlin in 1990 one year after the wall in Berlin was torn down with lot of well know musicians like the Scorpions, Van Morrison and Sinead OConnor. In the years 2011, 2012 and 2013 Roger Waters went on a world tour to perform The Wall in many cities around the world. I was lucky enough to witness a show in june 2011. The ticket was very expensive, but worth every single cent. Roger Waters had a great voice and was accompanied by talented musicians who were able to recreate The Wall and also the show was great and updated with new created video sequences.

Many People consider Pink Floyd's The Wall to be their Magnum Opus and greatest achievement. Needless to say I do not, I believe that honor rightfully falls to Dark Side Of The Moon. The Wall is full of individually astounding songs that are among the most loved, most played, and most influential of all time. There are no less than eight stone classic songs on The Wall, and that makes it a very good and valuable album indeed. It should also be said the The Wall is easily one of the greatest concept albums in Progressive Rock history.
