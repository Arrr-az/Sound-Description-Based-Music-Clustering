Ronald Reagan's America was the final severing of political discourse from reality, began (on a large scale) in the sixties with the overabundance of incoming information such that a value could no longer be placed on it. Don DeLillo once said that with the Kennedy assassination, 'reality became unmanageable.' The pieces didn't fit together because there were too many of them, and who is to tell which pieces belong to separate puzzles? The mass media influx of too much information all the time converted reality into white noise - so much to take in that no value can be attributed, leaving it all just static, moral relativity and social bargaining chips. Then came Reagan and 'morning in America' with a president suffering from senile dementia; whose make-believe storytelling was so grandfatherly that it was simply accepted as a new kind of emotional truth. What he said was true because it made people feel good and the public barriers between fiction and reality simply dissolved. What to do then as a disaffected suburban kid living voiceless in a soul-sucking flophouse known locally as 'the black hole'? Well, naturally, you start a hardcore band.

The Adolescents' debut is seminal - the nihilism of the American suburban youth writ large for all to see for the first time, with a primal ferocity and disinterest in success that separates them from those who followed in their footsteps (even though they stand as the only of the first wave of American Hardcore to score a hit single). This was a howl in the darkness. The howling is the act, whether anyone hears it is inconsequential. It is life as a zoo - a simulated habitat with tacit borders yet no one admits that those bars exist, or can agree on what the zookeepers' intentions are. Most of all, this is an album chronicling the boredom, frustration, and depression of being a teenager who does not (and does not want to) fit in. Intelligent, focused spew of hate and anger at the surrounding conservative society and the roving gangs of violent youth (while also examining their roots as the same stultifying boredom that is driving everyone else insane). The centerpiece is the brilliant five and a half minute "Kids of the Black Hole" depicting the hellish California punk youth lifestyle. Fucking essential.

CD reissue appends the Welcome to Reality EP, which is lesser but an interesting supposed next step that the band never ended up taking.

Although it kind of sounds dated now, this album is an all time west coast hardcore fucking classic. This band of outcasts defined the Orange County sound, with riffing guitars, solid bass, pounding drums, and snotty-as-fuck vocals. I Hate Children (And I Also Hate Everybody Else)
Adolescents' self-titled record perfects brash, snotty, juvenile punk. Themes of self-hatred and depravity reign supreme over thrashing guitars, ferocious drums and nimble bass-lines. Atop the chaos, vocalist Tony Cadena lashes out against a culture of mindless violence that he both partakes in and disdains (see "Word Attack").

This album defines what it means to be a forgotten, bored, cynical youth ready to kill. It wallows in its own self-hatred and in doing so condemns a society that offers no meaningful escape. Essential.

Innovative With Basic Themes
This is very similar in sound to “Milo Goes to College”, but with a different message behind it. This replaces the teenage angst with straight pessimism & more obvious political statements. So basically, the overdone themes of 80’s hardcore. However, it focuses more on attacking certain groups of people in Los Angeles, like on “L.A. Girl” & “No Friends”, which are about valley girls & people clinging onto surf culture.

Aside from those, there’s a couple great songs on here, namely “Kids of the Black Hole” & “Amoeba”. They both tell a genuinely good story that doesn’t stick to the basic thematic formula I mentioned before. The former does on occasion, but it does so in a creative way. Sadly, I didn’t get anything new out of this record. It just takes the pessimism of Dead Kennedys & the instrumentation/playfulness of Descendents and throws them together. At the time this sound was new (Descendents dropped a year later) & it would become massively influential, but I can’t say I’m huge on it.

5.75/10

Adolescents' self titled debut is among the most important wave of punk rock; no, not the "classic era" reigned by the Sex Pistols, but the hardcore, early 80s wave, fronted by groups like Black Flag and Dead Kennedys. This record particularly takes the playful nature of groups like Descendents and puts a uniquely potent, political twist on it. Whilst this album does lack variation from track-to-track, its clear to see how this record stands out from the crowd of the time: whether it be subversive songwriting methods or angsty performances, this record breaks conventions whilst still running true to the core of what made that hardcore punk era so unforgettable. Tracks like Amoeba even highlight the fact that snotty punk rock can be very much catchy and accessible, as that track remains the most successful and popular cut off the record. In its entirety, this release is a necessary addition to the canon of 80s punk, and i am certain it influenced many-a-band following it.

3.5 stars
