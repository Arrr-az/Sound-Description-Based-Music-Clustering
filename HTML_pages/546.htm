Torches Jun 08 2012
+3 Vote downBookmarkVote up
This was the album that showed The Offspring what they needed to do to be heard. Yes, that’s a bad thing; the universally huge “Pretty Fly (For a White Guy)” reached #1 in nine countries and repeated itself at least three times on each album to follow, and it’s the worst song on the album!

Still, saying it’s the worst obviously means that there are some better songs to be found here—in fact, a majority of the songs range from good to absolutely amazing.

The negativity is found thrice: in the novelty “Pretty Fly for a White Guy,” the boring “She’s Got Issues,” and “Why Don’t You Get a Job?”, which doubles as the hugest throwaway track I’ve ever heard, featuring horrible vocals, uncharacteristic instrumentals, and some sort of attempt at ska punk that falls flat on its face. “Feelings” and “Walla Walla” are just okay, but the bridge of “Walla Walla” is pretty cool.

The positivity is found in blistering pop punk songs like “No Brakes,” the cynical “Americana” foreboding a hellish future brought on by our culture, “The Kids Aren’t Alright,” the painfully depressing “The End of the Line,” “Have You Ever” with its slow, rhetorical ending, and “Staring at the Sun.”

“Pay the Man” is a golden moment in The Offspring’s career. Who would’ve thought that they could muster an eight-minute song that sounds so different from anything they’ve ever done, yet still finds some obscure, mystical, foreign way to rock? Everything about it is near flawless and beautiful.

Overall, it’s a decent album. The beginning of the end? Definitely. But don’t pass it up. It’s a pleasant pop punk experience with one of The Offspring’s greatest—and three of their worst.

HIGHLIGHTS: Have You Ever, Staring at the Sun, The End of the Line, Americana, Pay the Man

Fregilt Feb 02 2011
+1 Vote downBookmarkVote up
What's sad is that most people just judge this album on its leading single, "Pretty Fly." Whoever does really shouldn't; that's the ONLY song on the album like it, and even though it doesn't have the prettiest, punk-iest Offspring sound, you've got to admit... it's pretty clever.

Here's my rating by song:
1) Welcome - 0/5 - Let me count. They've had five of these, from Smash to Splinter. They just DON'T NEED it, just open up the album! I mean, at least the Ixnay one was clever...
2) Have You Ever - 5/5 - Flawless. It has a load of suspense and tension in it, a lot of cadences. This is one of The Offspring's finest songs.
3) Staring at the Sun - 3/5 - It's decent. It's a little boring and really repetitive, and they could have led Have You Ever into a better song.
4) Pretty Fly (For a White Guy) - 2/5 - Its only pro is the clever writing: "He needed some cool tunes, not just any would suffice, but they didn't have Ice Cube so he bought Vanilla Ice..." I mean, you can't say no to that. But this song is weak.
5) The Kids Aren't Alright - 4/5 - It's a good song, really. It has a good message; youth like me need to savor our lives and spend it the right way.
6) Feelings - 5/5 - Amazing cover of a terrible song. These guys have THE BEST covers. Check out their covers of "Next to You" and "Sin City" next.
7) She's Got Issues - 3/5 - Fun and REALLY catchy, and you'll end up listening to it several times in a row. Still, not a strong song. Just because it's catchy doesn't mean it's very great.
8) Walla Walla - 4/5 - Like "She's Got Issues," it's fun and catchy, and the bridge is probably one of my favorite bridges of all time, but... I dunno, it lacks something.
9) The End of the Line - 3/5 - You can tell here that they were trying to follow up one of their biggest hits, "All I Want," and they didn't do a good job at it. This song has a REALLY sad but good message, and it's alright, but it's just a bit boring.
10) No Brakes - 3/5 - It's loud, but Dexter tries to sing too high, and it comes out whiny and shriek-y. It's not very enjoyable, not just for this reason, but it's just too... something.
11) Why Don't You Get a Job? - 4/5 - It's fun and it's got classy Offspring ska. It's just a little annoying if listened to too often.
12) Americana - 5/5 - My third favorite from the album (after Have You Ever); this has an amazing message. Youth like me are spoiling ourselves. Plus, it has so much suspense and lots of unreleased tension, and it makes you want to scream, "Well F*CK YOU!" along with Dexter. This is an amazing song.
13) Pay The Man - 5/5 - THE BEST OFFSPRING SONG. Case closed. It's like Progressive Punk... man, that should be a genre. This song is purely amazing. I'm sure they'll never do anything like this again, which is a shame, but hey. We'll savor it while it's here, because this is an extremely good song, one of my favorites OF ALL TIME, too. This song is purely amazing. THE STRONGEST POINT OF THE ALBUM. The only shame is that "My life fits for me, oh, oh, oh" should have closed the album, not that Pretty Fly mariachi thing. That spoiled the suspenseful moment, just like on "Blood Sugar Sex Magik" with They're Red Hot at the end of Sir Psycho Sexy.

The average is 3.5-4 stars. When you get down to it, it's a great album, just a little weak in some areas and unreasonably strong in some, with strengths that they could have added to some of the weaker songs.

redhotbowieboy Jun 01 2023
Vote downBookmarkVote up
It is really hard to approach Pop-Punk without nostalgia. When listening to bands like The Offspring or Green Day, it is very easy to get lost in the power chords and catchy choruses and just reminisce about the "better days" while not really paying attention to the music at hand. That's how I felt about this album for a long time, in my head it was an untainted part of my childhood which if I went back to revisit, I'd surely hate it, right? I mean, this album has songs like Pretty Fly (For a White Guy) and Why Don't You Get a Job? this has to suck way more than i remember! Well, that is interestingly not the case for me with Americana. When coming back to this album after many years, instead of cringing at the songwriting and corny hooks, I actually found myself enjoying a lot of the drumming, guitars, and even Dexter's yell-y vocals. Americana seriously has some great hooks and riffs, Have You Ever, No Brakes, The End of the Line, Walla Walla, and the eternal classic The Kids Aren't Alright all have very catchy choruses, basslines, and guitarwork. The rest of the album isn't much different, with the only real misses being the aforementioned Pretty Fly, with its annoying as all hell hook and lyrics, and Why Don't You Get a Job? which I still cannot decide if it's terrible or pretty catchy, especially those drums! There are a few mediocre cuts on here, usually suffering from a flat hook or boring instrumentation, such as Feelings, Staring at the Sun, and She's Got Issues. That said, this album really boils down to a few amazing cuts that keep this from being a 3 or lower. Those being Americana, The Kids Aren't Alright, and Pay the Man. Americana is just the embodiment of Offsprings sound done right, with a soaring chorus and anthemic guitars that feel like being lifted out of a deep pit. The Kids Aren't Alright doesn't need much explanation, it has a good message about the youth savoring their golden years and not putting them to waste, the catchiest chorus on this album, and a guitar riff that lies dormant in the mind of every pop-punk kid like a sleeper agent dog-whistle. Finally, the closer Pay the Man is this really fucking weird and groovy prog(?)-punk song that has some cool progression and grooves that all culminate in a very explosive end section that tops the album off. Americana is a very dated album, it has many late 90s pop-punk cliches, many annoying vocal performances, cheesy lyrics, and not nearly enough of the raw self-loathing production and intensity the bands previous record, Smash had. I still had a lot of fun with this record though, it isn't perfect by any means but has lots of fun moments and even some smarter sections that keep me entertained.
