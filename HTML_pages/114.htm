A solid transitional effort. Sure, the less "mature" Kick Inside is nonetheless a lot more confident in what it is, and the two that followed this up are completely amazing, but this definitely shows how she got from point A to point B. I mean, something like "Delius" or "Blow Away" are more typical of Hounds of Love's second side than the Kate behind "Wuthering Heights" or "Wow," but you can still hear hints of that Kate in "Blow Away" - the orchestra behind her is still fairly standard chamber pop orchestration, and her voice isn't as rich as it would become in the future - "Egypt" has the loping fretless bass, ominous bridge, and exotic scales that characterize later Kate Bush, but her voice is still very much "Wuthering Heights."

And she hasn't fully shed the theatricality of her past, either. "Babooshka" might be her most forwardly sexual song up until this point (even when you don't factor the video in), and while it's great, with a gripping refrain and a cool, off-kilter melody, it doesn't quite have the emotional heft of her very best work - most of which, outside of "Wuthering Heights," "The Man with the Child in His Eyes," and a certain song on this album which I'll get to, came from The Dreaming onweards. Hell, "All We Ever Look For" is basically a show tune with a gurgling video game synthesizer. I don't like it very much, but it's not bad - it's just too close in spirit to one of the first two album's weaker tracks for me to fully enjoy it, the way I like a lot of other songs here. "The Wedding List," which combines music hall bounce with one of the few distorted guitars you'll ever hear on a Kate Bush record (you also get them on "Violin," which is as clumsy as "James and the Cold Gun" was cool and probably the worst song here), is a lot better, but she's not quite there yet. I do appreciate the C'era una volta il West [Era uma vez no Oeste] sample in there, though.

Despite this, there are several songs here where Kate's very definitely there. "Babooshka" is one of those songs, and the stretch from "The Infant Kiss" to "Breathing" has several of the best songs she's ever done. "The Infant Kiss" itself represents the full emergence of Kate-as-we-know-her, shedding the occasionally immature, overly precious nature of her early albums and coming up with a haunting, strange, impeccably arranged ghost story; its coda, "Night Scented Shock," is appropriately creepy. "Army Dreamers" has a terrific vocal arrangement, and its measured waltz tempo is cool by me; it's also basically the basis for early Joanna Newsom. And "Dreaming," the dramatic, apocalyptic, creepy closer, is fucking superb. The synthesizers, a new element to this album that would end up defining Kate, sound excellent, the bass solo/audio sample bit is awesome, and it's got one of Kate's most commanding vocals, with her getting more and more desperate as it goes on.

So it's a bit uneven, but it has a lot going for it - like a lot of other people have said, the diversity here is commendable, and Kate's always been a compelling storyteller in my book; on top of that, there are quite a few of her best songs here. The genius that would characterize her two big mid '80s albums is on its way, and in places it's there, but on a whole, she still had just a little bit of maturing to do before her full greatness emerged.

girlunstrung May 31 2006
+2 Vote downBookmarkVote up
This album could be considered The Dreaming as a toddler; the ideas are there but haven't been completely developed yet. Never for Ever was the first album that Kate really had control over and it straddles the line between the catchy pop songs of her first two releases and the arty unconventional songs on her next two. The result is mostly good with just a bit of bad.

The first single was opener "Babooshka" which is both catchy and weird. It tells the story of a woman whose husband doesn't notice her anymore so she disguises herself as a pretty young thing and sees what happens. "She wanted to test her husband/she knew exactly what to do/a pseudonym to fool him/she couldn't have made a worse move/she sent him scented letters/and he received them with a strange delight/just like his wife/but how she was before the tears/and how she was before the years flew by/and how she was when she was beautiful/she signed the letter 'all yours, Babooshka'..." The next few songs are your average pop songs and don't leave much of a lasting impression.

Now, we reach a 1-2-3 punch of weird and strangely addicting tracks. First is "Egypt", a sweeping and sultry ode to the Nile river, pyramids, and tombs. The end features an odd chant that keeps building and building along with some wind noises that turn this track into a near-epic. Next is one of my all-time Kate favorites: "The Wedding List", the story of a newly married couple who are victims of a random shooting. The husband dies and now the wife is out for revenge. This song is vicious and sinister and Kate yelps and snarls her way through it perfectly. Her voice is dripping with anger and hatred as she growls "I got him on the wedding list! I got him and I did not miss! I pinned him on the wedding list!" Finally, we get to "Violin" which can come off as annoying but is mostly just a lot of fun and very campy. She screeches and screams like some possessed banshee about her love for the instrument and makes us sure that she's gone completely bonkers.

"The Infant Kiss" is a short little ballad about an older woman's relationship/obsession with a young boy and features such creepy lyrics as "what is this/an infant kiss/that sends my body tingling/I've never fallen for a little boy before" and "I want to smack/but I hold back/I only want to touch". "Army Dreamers" is about a young soldier who died before he had a chance to live and features the genius background sound of a gun being cocked. The album ends on a high note with the epic "Breathing" about nuclear weapons from the point of view of a baby in the womb. ("Breathing my mother in/breathing my beloved in/breathing her nicotine/breathing the fallout in/out/in/out/in...")

This album is mostly amazing and gives us a few crazy songs so we aren't as jolted by the time The Dreaming comes around. This really establishes Kate as a true artist. She steps away from the screechy girlish vocals, though they are still there at times, and starts writing about more serious topics with less school-girl poetry lyrics. Overall, a great success.

Kate's first masterpiece; richly fantastical and all too brief.

Some major strides made here, as Kate has pretty much taken full control of the proceedings, and with considerable aplomb. Neck-and-neck with _The Dreaming_ for my top honors among Kate's albums, _Never for Ever_ kept all the best parts of her style and added a richer, nearly prog, sound and some even darker subject matter.

Kate's always had a darker side, and it manifests itself most vividly here on "The Wedding List." That track has a plot line that seems it could have been the inspiration for Quentin Tarantino's Kill Bill movies of today: man kills newlywed, lots of blood ("He swooned in maroon"), the bride swears revenge, lots more blood ("She's a widow, all in red, with his red, still wet"), bride gets revenge ("And when it's all over, you'll roll over, the butt of my gun, one in your belly and one for Rudi, you got what you gave by the heel of my bootie, Bang-Bang, out! Like an old cherootie, and I'm coming for you") Not exactly the plot line, but certainly bloody enough...

Kate really delivered a stunner here, and not one single song I'd skip. Matter of fact, this album gives my CD player's back button a real workout.

Bravo, Kate!

Compared to her first 2 albums, I would say this one is a huge improvement in terms of production and arrangements as I love the different instruments used on these tracks. Songwriting wise, I am not sure it is as consistent as her debut (which to me is still her best album of the first 3). This album is a delight to get through once I decided to skip ''Wedding List'' and ''Violin'' as I find her vocals can be pretty annoying on those tracks particularly. Song like ''Blown Away'' , ''All We Ever Looked For'' and ''Breathe'' all have moments I really like, and others not so much (which brings down the overall rating unfortunately) - Songs I think are excellent from beginning to end in terms of composition, performance and arrangement's are ''Babooshka'' , ''Egypt'' ,''Infant Kiss'' and ''Army Dreamers''.
