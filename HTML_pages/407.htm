I don't think a human being has ever, or will ever, make a song as beautiful as 'Pale Blue Eyes.' Maybe our new alien overlords will, but I highly doubt it. It seriously is one of the greatest songs ever, everything I want a song to be. As far as I'm concerned, The Velvet Underground are the most important band ever. I'd listened to The Velvet Underground & Nico and I'd listened to White Light/White Heat, I loved the former but hated the latter. But I wanted to listen to more, because even though I disliked White Light/White Heat at first, they were still a fascinating band. So I turned to their self titled album, and at first I had no idea that it was Caleless, and I believe he was the most important part about the band and he's what made them so ridiculously distinctive with those messy sprawling noisy arrangements, so when I sat down to listen to this - I was surprised at how quiet and thought out it was.

It's an album that really does show Lou Reed's importance to the band, because I'd always kind of underrated him due to Cale being so brilliant. But almost every song is brilliant, they're all constructed and crafted masterfully. My only complaint is 'The Murder Mystery', it shows Lou Reed can push boundaries, implement studio tricks and be just as wacky and weird without Cale, it doesn't belong on the album. It completely ruins the tone of an otherwise brilliant album, It's the only reason my rating for it isn't a little higher but despite that even though the dynamic of the band had changed they were still able to create something some believe to be a masterpiece and that's a truly wonderful thing. Also over time, I prefer the messy and weird White Light/White Heat to this one as well.

The Velvet Underground im Zentrum der Ruhe, so knnte man diese Platte beinahe darstellen. Im Oktober 1968 hatte Lou Reed seine beiden Mitstreiter Sterling Morrison und Maureen Tucker vor die Wahl gestellt, da man entweder den vierten Mann (und gleichzeitig zweiten Kreativpol), John Cale feuern solle oder er, Lou Reed selbst, wrde die Band verlassen. Auf dem Cover dieser dritten LP in der Band-Diskographie, sitzt Yule links auen und richtet seinen Blick auf das Zentrum des Bildes. Um das Zentrum postiert sitzen Tucker und Morrison, wobei der alte Sterl seinen Blick ins Off abwendet. Der Mittelpunkt des Coverphotos ist Lou Reed, der ein zrtlich, verletztes Lcheln aufsetzt. Lou Reed hatte sich mit seinem Affront gegen John Cale unter seinen Rekruten keinerlei Sympathien erworben, nein, eigentlich hatte er nur bewirkt, da er, nun als Alleinherrscher, losgelst gebieten konnte, doch die Einheit, die man zuvor zwei Alben lang gebildet hatte, war an ein Ende gelangt. It's almost like there's two different bands by the name, Velvet Underground. The one that everyone gets excited about and talks about and calls the fathers of like 50% of sub-genres (when its really they pioneered urban rock, no less an achievement but still). The Velvets of the amazing debut, and the Velvets of the overrated but still unforgettable White Light/White Heat. Then there's the Velvets of the last two albums, this one and Loaded, and boy howdy this is not the band that everyone gushes about. There's also a third Velvet Underground I guess, a fifth album with pretty much just Doug Yule...and it apparently sucks so we'll ignore that one. This second Velvet Underground! What the heck is it? Accordingly it has also become gushed over and "beloved" by many of the usual critical suspects. I don't know what the hell album they're all apparently listening to here, because they basically all seem to stretch their words to describe this as an extension of who they were before, continuing their amazing style and craziness. Except no, not really, not at all in fact. That first album? That's the Velvets I read about in the hype, the crazy cool band that sounded nothing like anyone else at the time and laid down incredibly important seeds that would be later watered and grown into huge trees by later bands. That Velvet is worth talks of "legend". This Velvet is just kind of a charming inconsequential lo-fi band that doesn't really have much to add to the world aside from a few more nice songs. I guess you could try and force yourself to make a description of this as HUGELY INFLUENTIAL TO LO FI STUFF FROM LATER except no way am I buying it. I don't know why it's apparently so tough for people to accept this as just very simple even irrelevant nice music. I think there's a fear in some critics that they have to throw 5 stars at every album an IMPORTANT band made. But as I've always maintained I don't think that helps anyone, it's no good for the band! When you give the full rating out so easily and casually to a bands whole work it diminishes the real peaks, and misleads your audience. There's nothing people hate more than a constantly overrated work that they feel confused on for what they're "missing". But an appropriate review? That better sets folks up for what they're getting, gives the album a more open audience. And the Velvets? After losing John Cale? They're really not much more than a simple band that wants to basically make pop music. Members at the time were almost relieved that the crazy times were behind them, and wanted to get noticed and make good sales. Cale aside? That was always a part of them, wierdos sure, but wierdos who just wanted to be in a band and do band stuff. Only one song on here would I even describe as strange, The Murder Mystery, which ...is the best song on here? But it doesn't define anything here. Instead it's slow strummy songs like Candy Says and the insanely cute closing song After Hours sung by the drummer chick Maureen Tucker. Do I miss Cale? Do I think they should have kept making noise backed experiments and become Sonic Youth some fifteen years early? Well yeah, but then again I almost think what Cale was trying to do with the band was almost too ahead of its time. The world (and rest of the band!) was not urbanized enough in mind or place to push that frontier all the way. I'm saying that it wasn't to be, no matter how wishful we get. And that this relaxed chill version of the band is a fine way for these poor souls to wind down. Poor pioneers, too confused and ignored to continue the thankless fight. You did not get ignored in vain!
