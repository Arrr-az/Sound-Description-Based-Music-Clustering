Perfection. Ditches the dull, futile rock/jazz/classical approach of Lizard and Islands and finally pioneers that classic King Crimson sound - Avant-Garde METAL (or, if I'm going to make up a cooler name, "Avant-HARD" or "ExperiMETAL") with twisted dissonant evil jazz guitars, confounding mathematical time signature changes and awesome instrumental interplay. Post-Rock and Math Rock as we know them pretty much began here.

The opening track, part one of the title track, seamlessly flies through ambience, jazz fusion, metal and horror movie music. Fripp goes completely insane on his guitar, going straight from beefy Black Sabbath riffing to convoluted atonal hyper-fast math-rock jazz whatever. John Wetton and David Cross showcase some pretty dazzling virtuosity too, not to mention the dense dual percussion assault: Prog rock's number one drummer Bill Bruford and avant-garde percussion weirdo Jamie Muir (who quit and became a monk after this album).

Next up there's Book of Saturday, a beatiful little ballad that serves as a nice breather in the middle of the experimental mayhem. Exiles and Easy Money are both spacy, slow-burning jams - sort of like a weirder Pink Floyd. And they save the best for last: The Talking Drum starts almost completely silent and slowly builds into a dense, feverish noise orgy. It finally explodes with a scream, going straight into the last and greatest track: Larks' Tongues in Aspic Part II, one of King Crimson's most famous and popular songs. It's an utterly weird but hard rocking instrumental that repeats awesome riffs and intense melody for a slow, almost mathemetical build-up into an epic climax. I actually counted the time signature changes in this song, and there's more than 20 of them.

Larks' Tongues in Aspic is a unique, influential classic. While it's not THE best King Crimson album, it's a must for any serious prog fan.

I love every single second of this album. Larks' Tongues in Aspic is one of my favourite progressive rock albums there is. With this record King Crimson experimented with a slightly harder sound. This is often called as their heaviest album and it's no surprise. Especially the opener and the closing track have some very heavy parts here and there.

But even if the sound is a bit heavier than usually the songs are still delivered with the classic King Crimson style and class. There are six songs in here and three of those are instrumentals with a huge leaning towards both jazz fusion and experimental rock. There is no point in trying to pick up some highlights here because the album works best as a whole damn thing.

While this ain't as famous album as their groundbreaking debut is I think this is just as great musically. This album has everything a progressive rock fanatic could want from the high class instrumental work to beautiful melodies and mighty/epic sounds. This album has it all and in my opinion it's totally flawless.

After the end of the Islands tour, Robert Fripp was once again left with the task of reconstructing King Crimson's lineup from scratch. However, rather than continue down the path of trying to produce a symphonic followup worthy of In the Court of the Crimson King, Fripp took the more daring approach: he wouldn't just create a new lineup, he'd break down and rebuild what it meant to be King Crimson from the ground up.

In the Court of the Crimson King kickstarted a new genre of progressive rock and was immediately embraced by the prog community, who soon took its lessons to heart. Larks' Tongues In Aspic comes up with its own genre yet again, and decades later the rest of the music world still hasn't caught up to it, except for perhaps a few bands right on the cutting edge of prog or math rock/post-rock. With angular rhythms, avant-garde percussion, Bill Bruford unleashed to try out jazzy chops that had been suppressed in Yes, John Wetton providing the best vocals and basswork on a King Crimson album since Greg Lake left, David Cross adding a plaintive and enigmatic violin to the proceedings, and Fripp laying down some of the angriest and heaviest riffs seen on a rock album to date, the album introduces the mid-1970s Crimson lineup (around the rock-solid core of Wetton, Fripp and Bruford) with a true tour de force.

Easily the best King Crimson album since their debut, this is the album which reinvented the band, and in doing so reinvented rock music altogether, and it still yields secrets with repeated listens to this day. If you only like symphonic prog and have no love for the heavier, more avant-garde end of prog, maybe this isn't for you, but otherwise if you like King Crimson, you need this album. Like In the Court of the Crimson King and Discipline, it's one of the key puzzle pieces that's essential to putting the picture together; if you don't taste the Aspic, you don't know King Crimson.

The platform from which Robert Fripp launched such memorable musical experimentations as 'Starless and Bible Black' and 'Red'; in fact, one could say 'Lark's Tongue' was the first of a trilogy of albums with like objectives. Yet, for all the furious fretwork and metallic moments of the album 'Red', it is merely the logical outcome of the ideas derived from 'Lark's Tongue in Aspic', save that the latter maintains the sense of harmony and acoustic passages from earlier Crimson albums, such as 'In the Court of the Crimson King'.

In addition, David Cross's violin and viola add another intriguing layer to the musical mix, particularly the passages of the instumental "Lark's Tongue in Aspic, Part I" that were influenced by Ralph Vaughan William's classical composition "Lark Ascending". Three superb vocal tracks, "Book of Saturday", "Exiles" and "Easy Money" feature bassist John Wetton singing the lyrics of Richard Palmer-James (formerly of Supertramp). Lark's Tongue in Aspic is a remarkable synthesis of progressive rock, jazz-fusion, heavy metal, orientalism and classical composition, full of surprising and exotic percussion by Jamie Muir and Bill Bruford, and the biting, eccentric guitarwork of Robert Fripp, who influenced a wide array of bands, such as Tool, Nirvana, Mudvayne, Iron Maiden, Porcupine Tree, Voivoid and Bad Religion.

Influences aside, 'Larks Tongue in Aspic" Makes you wish the music of 1973 was more fashionable in these decadent days of compositional squalor.

Lark`s tongues in aspic es, indudablemente, otro genial lanzamiento de King Crimson. Las opiniones de esta obra son muy variadas: algunos aman este disco y lo consideran uno de los mejores trabajos del Rey armes, mientras otros lo aborrecen.
El estilo avant-garde es llevado a su pico y de una manera espectacular: composiciones vanguardistas mucho ms disfrutables que las de Islands aparecen en Larks tongues in aspic. Si el anterior disco se destacaba por su variedad, sus hermossimos arreglos y sus perfectos ambientes, ste disco brilla por su genial manera de interpretar y hacer verdaderamente disfrutables temas experimentales. Pero la vanguardia no es el nico estilo que aparece en este LP: aqu se encuentran los pasajes ms trasheros de King Crimson.
Los percusionistas ahora son dos. Bill Bruford est en la batera, pero adems, est Jamie Muir tocando los accesorios de una genial manera, aportando pequeos detalles que hacen el sonido del grupo ms propio de este. La banda se desarma completamente: entra David Cross a cargo de los violines, John Wetton a cargo de la voz y el bajo y Fripp queda como compositor, guitarrista y tecladista. Richard Palmer Jones reemplaza a Peter Seinfeld en las letras, y esto marca la primera formacin en la que no hay ningn miembro originario del grupo (exceptuando Fripp, por supuesto).
Los ambientes presentados en este disco son en general misteriosos, oscuros y pesados. Pero tambin hay toques de dulzura y tintes del estilo semejante al rock-bluescultivado en el anterior disco. Pero vayamos a los temas.

Larks tongues in aspic Pt. 1 comienza con un crescendo de percusin, cuerdas y guitarras suaves, que luego es roto completamente por una meloda trash de guitarra, acompaada por un Bill Bruford ensaado con sus tambores. Pero lo mejor viene cuando Fripp ejecuta sus frenticas, locas y rpidas melodas que nos demuestran que adems de ser un guitarrista con un gusto musical excepcional capaz de hacernos llorar con dos notas, es tambin un instrumentista dotado del don de tocar rpido. Este tema contiene uno de los pasajes instrumentales ms grandes de Robert. El desastre se conforma definitivamente, en un espacio libre de improvisacin para cualquier msico. Cabe destacar el excelente aporte de David Cross: cuando el volumen baja, l improvisa tajantemente y agrega un matiz totalmente nuevo en el sonido de King Crimson: el violn. El final es digno de un maestro: los rganos atmosfricos acompaados de las siniestras guitarras constituyen un gran cierre.
En Book of Saturday es solamente disfrutable: una meloda linda y un acompaamiento agradable, nada ms. Una de las cosas destacables son las extraas y hermosas notas ejecutadas por la guitarra, que tienen el majestuoso efecto que se presentaba en el estribillo de The letters (como amo ese sonido).
Exiles es un rock acaramelado con violines romnticos y melodas destacables. El ambiente que crea sta cancin es definitivamente el ms dulce del disco. Adems, es el nico tema con guitarra acstica, con grandiosos arpegios desplegados por Fripp. Tal vez haya un solo defecto aqu: la voz de Wetton. El pobre alcanza a duras penas las notas tan agudas, cosa que hace que se escuche un sonido spero en su voz.
En Easy Money como una marcha, se acercan acordes cargados de vigor, unos buensimos gritos, y una autoritaria batera. La meloda no es tan genial como otras, pero sin embargo es disfrutable, pero las improvisaciones de Fripp constituyen el fuerte del tema, confromando uno de los mejores solos de Robert
The talking drum es otro avant-garde que, haciendo la excepcin, es el ms aburrido del disco. El riff de bajo es espectacular, pero montono. Aunque esto es suplantado por el violn maravilloso de Cross y por los variados y bien trabajados matices de la cancin.
Larks tongues in aspic Pt. 2 es otra composicin vanguardista maravillosa que contiene diferentes secciones, una mejor que otra, con bestiales improvisaciones y gigantes y trasheras melodas de guitarra. Todo aqu se destaca: los violines, las guitarras, la percusin y el bajo son excelentes, y no dejan de fascinarme. Adems, es el tema en el que ms se destaca el trabajo de los bateristas.

Nunca he escuchado composiciones vanguardistas tan geniales como las que contiene este LP. Adems, Larks tongues in aspic marca definitivamente la segunda etapa de los 70s de King Crimson: el avant-garde queda impregnado en la mente de Fripp, y eso se puede ver en los siguientes trabajos del grupo.


Puntuacin: 9+

Mejor tema: Lark's tongues in aspic Pt. 2

Highlights: Lark's tongues in aspic Pt. 1; Exiles; Easy Money; Lark's tongues in aspic Pt. 2.
