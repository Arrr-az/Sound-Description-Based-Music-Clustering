Migration...
This was the second album I bought in 2017 where my first impressions have been 'this is so bad, it might actually be good'...

Many listens in I actually favour a more generous and positive opinion of 'Everything Now'. And yes of course it's absolutely true that Arcade Fire have migrated from indie rock to something far more art rock. Indeed as the genres in the RYM headings suggest; alternative dance, synth pop, disco, new wave. Whether they migrate back to the grandeur of 'Neon Bible' is yet to be seen.

For the meantime, I am actually more than happy with 'Everything Now', and for sure I much prefer it to 'Reflektor'.

For such a big drop in rating, I don't dislike this at all, it feels like a crazy turn from what I expect from Arcade Fire, but I think it works great. The vocals are good, I like Will Butler's (I think) voice a lot, it has a nice nasally quality that isn't overdone and feels consistent across the entire album, the writing may not be the most creative or interesting, but its dynamic enough to work for the album and it mingles with the instrumentals quite a bit. Speaking of the instrumentals, I think they were fairly solid on this album, they definitely hit all the key components of modern disco and synth, the diverse instrumental mix of electric, electronic, and acoustic was well balanced, and every instrument felt pretty funky and fun when it tried to, and pretty solid otherwise even if it wasn't technically the most skilled. The highlights were Creature Comfort, Electric Blue, and Put Your Money on Me, while the lowlights were Infinite Content and Infinite_Content, both for being repetitive and generally annoying with the vocals, although I do think the idea of having two mirrored tracks like this is interesting. The album cover is a blatant copy of Maroon 5's album V, that being said it works well with the purple neon and the desert sunset, and I like the weird textured look of the sky. Overall, really not bad at all, its nice modern disco and synthpop with a few stinkers thrown in, but I think the highlights outweigh the lowlights and the album comes out as generally enjoyable.

Arcade Fire a la venta
Otro disco difícil de analizar para mí, no tanto por su calidad en sí o por las controversias actuales de la banda (que hacen difícil seguir escuchándolos directamente), sino porque está profundamente conectado con un momento de mi vida personal que representó un gran cambio. De la misma manera esto puede verse en Arcade Fire durante esta era, y si bien ya venían denotando interés por géneros como el synthpop y el nu-disco, en este álbum redoblaron la apuesta con según yo algunos de sus mejores carácteres (la apertura con el tema homónimo "Everything Now"). Por otra parte hay aspectos que se les van mucho de la mano como la inconsistencia en la calidad compositiva y lírica de track en track, con algunas performances como la vocal de Regine en "Electric Blue" que literalmente me hace doler los oídos. Un trabajo disparejo pero ambicioso y que coincide con su momento de mayor éxito comercial lo cual va en sintonía con el concepto del disco.
